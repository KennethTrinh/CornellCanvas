Great!! The potential and kinetic energy densities at a given position are generally not equal in a standing wave (for example at the instance the string is flat, the potential energy density is zero everywhere, but the kinetic energy density is large, since the string is moving fastest at that instant), so A is not true. Energy does not propagate along the string from one end to the other end in a standing wave...this only happens in a traveling wave, so B is also not true. The string sections at the anti-nodes oscillate with the largest amplitude in the standing wave, but at the instances these sections reach their maximum displacement, the transverse speed is zero, and so the kinetic energy density becomes zero. So C is wrong too. That leaves Answer D: The total energy in the standing wave oscillates between potential and kinetic energy. At an instance when the string is flat, potential energy density is zero (the string is not stretched), but kinetic energy density is large (since the string is moving fast). At an instance when the standing wave reaches its maximum displacement, kinetic energy density is zero, but potential energy density is large. So the total energy (which is constant) oscillates between kinetic and potential energy.
