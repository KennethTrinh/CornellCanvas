To increase the angular size of the image .
exact_answer none 455003 28863
margin of error +/-
29782
To produce an image distant from the eye, that can be observed with the eye muscles relaxed.
To produce an image distant from the eye, that can be observed with the eye muscles relaxed.
exact_answer none 455003 29782
margin of error +/-

Correct! Although more and more optical instruments use high resolution sensors to record images, which are then viewed on a separate electronic screen, many still allow direct viewing of images by eye.  The eye itself functions like a camera: the cornea and lens create an inverted real image on the retina, with focusing accomplished by small distortions of the lens.  The eye is most comfortable looking at distant objects. The eyepiece lens is thus positioned to generate a distant and highly magnified virtual image of the image formed by the objective lens. So the eyepiece purpose is not to create a real image. That is the job of the eye of the observer.
