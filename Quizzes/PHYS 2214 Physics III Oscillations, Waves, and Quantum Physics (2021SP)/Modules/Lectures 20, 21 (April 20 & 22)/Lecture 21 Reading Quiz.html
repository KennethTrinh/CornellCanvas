Correct! A particle fundamentally can not have an arbitrarily well defined position and an arbitrarily well defined momentum at the same time, but for a given particle one can either measure the position or its momentum with as high of a precision as the instrument used allows. Note however, that when either position or momentum of a quantum particle are measured, the wave function of the particle changes, so its properties are changed! For example, when you measure the position of a particle with high precision, you know where the particle is at that time, so it now has a small uncertainty in position, but its uncertainty in momentum will be large after the position measurement.
