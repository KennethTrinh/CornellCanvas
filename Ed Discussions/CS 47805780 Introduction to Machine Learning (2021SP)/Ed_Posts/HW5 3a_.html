This can be a tricky thing to prove in general, and often requires some deeper intuition about the structure of your hypotheses. I would recommend looking at the diagram we gave you (or any such pair of rectangles) and first locating the decision boundaries i.e. what is separating positive and negative points; then consider a point near one of these boundaries and whether you are able to adjust that boundary to give that point a different label. Hopefully that points you in the right direction for forming your whole set -- if you can adjust the boundaries as such to independently affect the label of each of your points, then your set is shatterable.
