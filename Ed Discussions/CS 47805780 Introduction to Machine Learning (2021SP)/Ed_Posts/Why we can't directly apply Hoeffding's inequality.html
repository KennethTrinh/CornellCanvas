From the wiki:

In probability theory , Hoeffding's inequality provides an upper bound on the probability that the sum of bounded independent random variables deviates from its expected value by more than a certain amount.

And we also have that: $$err_{S}(\hat{h}) = \frac{1}{m}\Sigma_{i=0}^{m}\Delta(y_{i}, \hat{h}(x_{i}))$$

So, $err_{S}(\hat{h})$ is a sum of bounded independent random variables so long as $\Delta(y_{i}, \hat{h}(x_{i}))$ is an independent RV . But, the $h_{S}$ that you get after doing ERM is using this loss function to choose the best hypothesis on the data. So, once you've picked the $h_{S}$ by minimizing the loss function, you've removed the randomness from the equation, breaking one of the assumptions needed for the Hoeffding inequality.
