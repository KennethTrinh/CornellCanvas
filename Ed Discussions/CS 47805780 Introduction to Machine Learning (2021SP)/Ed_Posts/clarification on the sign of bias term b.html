The b of the equation w1x1 + w2x2 = b is not necessarily the same as the bias term b for the linear classifier definition (just as w1 and w2 do not necessarily mean the same thing as the weight vector w). It could also have been written as "give its equation in the form yx1 + zx2 = c". This equation is simply the "standard form" for the equation of a line (vs slope-intercept, point-slope etc.). Part of your task for this question is to relate the standard form of the line back to the parameters of the linear classifier. b in the equation is just part of expressing its format and, as you pointed out, is not meant to be exactly the same as the bias term (but how might the two be related?)
