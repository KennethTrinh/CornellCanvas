I just wanted to clarify -- if our stride parameter is larger than 1, does that mean that our convolution matrix no longer has dimension (n-f+1)/(n-f+1), but would have smaller dimensions than this, since we will end up performing a lesser number of sums of elementwise multiplications between the larger matrix and filter matrix?

Yes, I believe the rule (assuming no padding) is

\dim\left(output\right)=\frac{n-f}{s}+1

where s is the stride. This agrees with the dimensions in the lecture when s = 1 .
