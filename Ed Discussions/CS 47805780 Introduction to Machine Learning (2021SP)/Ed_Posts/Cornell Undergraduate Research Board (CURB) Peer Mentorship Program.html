This sounds pretty interesting, so I am posting it here: ----------------- Dear Professor Joachims, My name is Phil Martinez and I am the co-chair of the Cornell Undergraduate Research Board (CURB) Peer Mentorship Program. CURB is recruiting students for our Peer Mentorship Program, and we would be extremely grateful if you could share the information below about our research opportunities with your students. I have also attached images, if you would like to share them with students in class or on Canvas. The Cornell Undergraduate Research Board (CURB) is currently recruiting both mentors and mentees for our Peer Mentorship Program (PMP). Students from ALL backgrounds and fields are encouraged to apply. -As a mentee in the Peer Mentorship Program, students will be paired with a mentor in their field of interest and learn how to get involved in research at Cornell through weekly workshops and presentations. -As a mentor in the Peer Mentorship Program, students will have a chance to hone their leadership skills as they assist a group of mentees in their paths to finding research. -Students interested in applying to be a mentee can do so here tinyurl.com/MenteeCURB2021 -Students interested in applying to be mentor can do so here tinyurl.com/MentorCURB2021 The deadline to apply for as a mentor is February 13th at 11:59 p.m. The deadline to apply as a mentee is February 20th at 11:59 p.m. More information can be found on our website, curb.cornell.edu. Thank you so much in advance! Questions? Email curbpmp@cornell.edu!
