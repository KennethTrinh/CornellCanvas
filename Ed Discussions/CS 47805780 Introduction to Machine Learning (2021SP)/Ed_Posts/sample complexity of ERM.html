This is simply using a physical analogy for our hypotheses' errors, where falling inside the region represents an error and falling outside the region represents a correct classification. What this is trying to show is that the mistake examples may or may not overlap, and the physical analogy is only meant to provide intuition, not any sort of proof.

Author: Michael Noor (staff)

This is just continuing that same physical analogy. Err_p is the true probability of an error, which corresponds to how large the region is relative to the total area (or how likely a new rock is to fall into the error region), whereas Err_s is the observed error rate, which corresponds to what fraction of the rocks that were actually thrown happened to land in the region (i.e. happened to actually get an error in practice).
