Author: Andrew Dettmer (staff)

Vote Count: 1

Visually, I like to imagine the hyperplane as my open palm. If I stick out my left hand flat, then put my right hand on it with my thumb sticking straight out away from my palm, the weight vector would be my thumb. Points lying on my palm are defined by the weight vector given by my thumb (with a dot product = 0), as well as the point in 3D space from which my thumb starts (bottom of my right hand laying on my left palm). If the origin were at the floor directly below my hands, the negative of the bias term would be the distance from the floor to my hands. Hope that helps ! (happy to dive into it further if there are more specific things that you are confused about or if the visual was not helpful)

Author: Michael Noor (staff)

Vote Count: 0

Effectively, the weight vector decides how much weight is given to each feature in deciding the decision boundary. The higher w_i is, the more influence x_i has on the x's classification.
