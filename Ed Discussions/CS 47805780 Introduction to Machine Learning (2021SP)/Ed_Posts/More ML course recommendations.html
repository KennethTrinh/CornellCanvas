This is such a great question, and while I can't speak for all of course staff, I can give my perspective. From the undergrad course offering:

- CS 4670 CV focuses on the CNNs and vision we didn't really touch-- this is my favorite subfield of ML so I might be biased but it's a very good intro.

- CS 4740 with Cardie is a course that will teach you a lot about NLP and from my friends perspective the projects are challenging but rewarding.

- CS 4786 (if offered) seems to have been a great course for unsupervised methods and practical applications.

- CS 4787 is a fantastic course that builds directly off of 4780 (almost like a part 2) and De Sa is an electric professor.

- CS 4789 is a new course (focusing on RL) but have heard very good things. Rather math heavy in comparison. Reinforcement learning is a really neat kind of ML.

Random Ones You Might Not Think Of

- CS 4220 is a math course but has many things that apply to ML

- INFO 4300 is a hidden gem of a course taught by DNM iirc that

There are courses on top of the ones I listed that deal with ML that I've left out, intentionally and unintentionally, but in the list above you will find some awesome semesters.
