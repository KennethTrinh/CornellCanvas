1) The $C\vec{w}\leq \vec{a}$ is for the general form of a Linear Program (it represents the idea of a nonstrict inequality). It doesn't necessarily have to be $\leq$, it could just as well be $\geq$, which is the constraint we use in the green box. In our case, $\vec{u}$ doesn't really matter - we're not necessarily trying to maximize anything at the moment, just classify everything correctly. That's why that part is crossed out; it's technically part of a linear program, but for our purposes it's not that relevant. 2) $\vec{w}$ and $\vec{x}$ need to have the same dimensions so that we can take their dot product. Hope that helped!
