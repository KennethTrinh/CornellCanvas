Stay tuned for gradient descent, it will be coming up shortly! You could read ahead in the UML textbook about Gradient Descent (chapter 14 in my version). One of the properties of convex optimisation problems is that local solutions are also globally optimal. (Think: finding the minimum of a parabola like $x^2 + x + 2$). Convex problems are generally "feasible" to solve efficiently- general mathematical optimisation is NP-Hard. For a lot more intuition and detail, check out chapter 12 of the UML textbook on "Convex Learning Problems" (again, the chapter number might be different in my version).
