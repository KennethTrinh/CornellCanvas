Here, f is the probability distribution function for an exponential distribution.

The conditional distribution for the observed n draws is given by

f\left(x_1,x_2,...,x_n\ |\ \lambda\right)

If the distribution is assumed to be iid , the likelihood would be

f\left(x_1,x_2,...,x_n\ |\ \lambda\right)=\Pi_{i=1}^nf\left(x_i\ |\ \lambda\right)

And the given probability distribution function can be substituted and manipulated to give the maximum likelihood estimate, similar to the problem on page 12.

f is a probability mass function, i.e. given lambda and x, what is the probability that the random variable X comes out to x. The exp(-lambda dot x) term is such that, as x increases the probability of that x being chosen gets exponentially smaller, and the lambda controls how quickly it decays. Since the fs are already probabilities, a sigmoid would not be beneficial.
