The answer above is great!

There are many correct answers. The hint refers to another one -- consider a hypothesis class that has one parameter (like a threshold, or the radius of a circle centered on the origin). And, this one parameter is determined by "merging" M parameters -- like taking the largest of the M parameters, or taking their sum or their average etc.

e.g. a circle centered on the origin whose radius is the largest of M parameters

Author: Jane Du (staff)

Vote Count: 0

If you have 2 points in 2d space, either they're equally far from the origin, or one is farther away. In the first case, you cannot include one point in the circle and exclude the other, so you cannot shatter a set of 2 points. In the second case, you cannot shatter either -- you cannot include the farther point inside the circle without also including the first.
