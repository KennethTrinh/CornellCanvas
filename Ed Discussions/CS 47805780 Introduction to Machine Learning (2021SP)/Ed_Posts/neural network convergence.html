This is a problem that you will rerun into over and over again in ML. The general solution (as a best-of-practice method) is to choose the highest learning rate that allows for your model to converge and then train as long as you can for train loss to go to 0. The latter thought was combated a bit recently because long you train -> more you overfit; however, recent literature like https://openreview.net/pdf?id=B1g5sA4twr has exposed there also exists a double descent catch to this kind of thinking. To answer shortly: 1000 epochs is not way too many epochs from my perspective. No such thing as too many epochs. If your training objective is continuing to go down, train longer!
