Author: Jane Du (staff)

Vote Count: 0

You don't need to create a variable that comes "from" xTr. How would you even do it, when you don't have a hyperplane?

If you had and optimal w and xTr, you could calculate xi, and if you had optimal xi and xTr, you could calculate w. But you don't have both w and xi, so you optimize for both, knowing that any w/xi that satisfy the constraints will logically represent what you want them to represent.
