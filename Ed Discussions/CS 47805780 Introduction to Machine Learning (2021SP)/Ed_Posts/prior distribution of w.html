The way I like to think about this is that given no knowledge at all about the data we would think of w as some random vector close to zero. Each data point gives us a little more information about the size and direction of w. Since ws far from 0 are extremely unlikely given our initial assumption about w, it won't be made large unless the data REALLY wants us to.

If you were to draw the probability as a function of w there would be a single f axis and an "x-axis" for each entry in w, and it would look like a multi-dimensional generalization of a bell curve (which is difficult to picture for w of length more than 2).
