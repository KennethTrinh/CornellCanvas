If $t > \frac{R^2}{\gamma ^2}$ then $\sqrt{t} >= \frac{R}{\gamma}$ Hence we know that:

$\sqrt{t}\frac{\gamma}{R} >= 1$

Then putting t+1 we get:

$\sqrt{t+1}\frac{\gamma}{R} > 1$
