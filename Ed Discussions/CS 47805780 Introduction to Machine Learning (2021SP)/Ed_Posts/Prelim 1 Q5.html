Why is it that the geometric margin is at least $2/ \sqrt{20 \times 1 + 9}$ and not equal to it? I.e. why do we need the weight vector $\vec{w}$ to have a functional margin $\gamma = 1$ for us to conclude that the geometric margin is $1/ ||\vec{w}||$?

------------------------------------

Author: Michael Noor (staff)

Vote Count: 1

This is because we may or may not actually end up with points sitting on that margin, so if we have a nice dataset it's larger. For the geometric margin, it's equal to whatever the functional margin is divided by the euclidean norm of the weight vector (since the larger the magnitude of the weight vector, the larger the corresponding functional margin).
