Unfortunately, I have to agree with Michael that this sort of opaque notation is something that can't be avoided. I usually look to online resources first to set up a baseline understanding before tackling the textbook's descriptions, which helped a lot during the course. It might also be useful to make sure you never read it without a pen and paper nearby, to walk through every step yourself - while skimming is tempting, you'll soon be overwhelmed by formulas if you don't break everything down as soon as it shows up and ensure that at least the only confusing thing on every line will be the new material.
