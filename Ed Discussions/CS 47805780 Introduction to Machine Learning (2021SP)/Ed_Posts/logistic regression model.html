$$1 - sigm(\vec{w}, \vec{x}) = \frac{\exp(-\vec{w}\cdot\vec{x})}{1 + \exp(-\vec{w}\cdot\vec{x})} = \frac{\exp(-\vec{w}\cdot\vec{x}) \cdot \exp(+\vec{w}\cdot\vec{x})}{\exp(+\vec{w}\cdot\vec{x}) + \exp(-\vec{w}\cdot\vec{x}) \cdot \exp(+\vec{w}\cdot\vec{x})} = \frac{1}{1 + \exp(+\vec{w}\cdot\vec{x})} = \frac{1}{1 + \exp[-(-\vec{w}\cdot\vec{x})]} = sigm(-\vec{w}, \vec{x}) $$
