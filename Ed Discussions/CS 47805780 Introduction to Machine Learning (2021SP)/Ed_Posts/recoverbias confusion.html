Yes, you're supposed to pick a specific a_i that's as far as possible from C and 0, and you should also use the x_i and y_i that correspond to that a_i. Once you've selected the x_i and y_i you want, you can rearrange the equation below to solve for b.

y_i(\sum_{j=1}^na_j\ y_j\ k(x_j,\ x_i)+b)=1

For the summation, you're supposed to sum up (a_j * y_j * k(x_j, x_i)), for j = 1 through j=n, where n is the total number of support vectors. You can do this using numpy operations.
