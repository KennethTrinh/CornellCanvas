You do not use the same formula in both questions.

On slide for #1098, both of the fomulas are the same, the second formula is just the first formula solved for m.

For the first question, the general sample complexity formula applies, as you can see on the slides. #1048 explains why you need to use this formula, rather than using Hoeffding's inequality directly. Also, #998 explains why calculating the probability of $err_{s}(h_{s})$ - $err_{p}(h_{s}) > \epsilon /2$ is the same as the probability of $err_{p}(h_{s})$ - $err_{p}(h^{*})$ $>$ $\epsilon$, which is why the general sample complexity formula is helpful here.

To answer part ii, $h_{friend}$ is a random hypothesis (rather than one you selected using training), which means you do not need to do the union bound.
