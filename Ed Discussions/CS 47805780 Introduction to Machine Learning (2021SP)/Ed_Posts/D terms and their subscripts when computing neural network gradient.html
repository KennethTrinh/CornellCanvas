Here, "d" is the depth of the network. So, D_d is just D_l where l = d. In the above formulas, the first one shows you how to compute D_d (i.e. the D value at the last layer), and the second one shows you how to compute D_l given that you've already computed D_l+1. So in order to compute all the D values, you start by computing D_d, and then loop backwards through your layers and compute D_l using the recursive formula. Because you're looping backwards, D_l+1 will always be computed by the time you get to computing D_l (and you stop once you've computed D_l where l = 0).
