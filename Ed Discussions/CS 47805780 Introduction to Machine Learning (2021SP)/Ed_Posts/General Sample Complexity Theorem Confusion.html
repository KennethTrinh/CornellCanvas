We know that if $err_{s}(h_{s})$ - $err_{p}(h_{s})$ $\leq$ $\epsilon /2$, and $err_{s}(h^{*})$ - $err_{p}(h^{*})$ $\leq$ $\epsilon /2$, then $err_{p}(h_{s})$ - $err_{p}(h^{*})$ $\leq$ $\epsilon$ (see the 2 slides before).

When $err_{s}(h_{s})$ - $err_{p}(h_{s})$ $>$ $\epsilon /2$, then we know the conditions in the previous sentence are false, and the probability of that happening is upper bounded by Pr($err_{s}(h_{s})$ - $err_{p}(h_{s})$ $>$ $\epsilon /2$), which is itself upper bounded by Hoeffding inequality on the RHS.

So we know that RHS bounds Pr($err_{p}(h_{s})$ - $err_{p}(h^{*})$ $>$ $\epsilon$), i.e. the probability that the difference between $err_{p}(h_{s})$ and $err_{p}(h^{*})$ is large is small.
