Our optimization problem started as a minimization of our objective function, but then, once we introduced our Lagrange multipliers, we ended up adding a maximization to our objective function (now summed up with the constraints). How do we know that we want to maximize this resulting objective function?

Author: Michael Noor (staff)

Basically what's going on here is that the outer min is choosing a w such that (1-y_i(w*x_i)) <= 0 for all i, else you could pick an infinitely large alpha_i for the corresponding point i; effectively a_i is just encoding an enforcement of the linear constraint without the use of a s.t. term.

Wait, so do we want to choose a small or large alpha_i? Also, why do we add the "max" into the optimization problem?

Author: Michael Noor (staff)

Vote Count: 0

The max will either choose infinite or 0 alpha, depending on the value of the rest of the term, which is what forces the min to choose a good w. The max is effectively just the algorithm's way of encoding the y(wx+b)>=... constraint implicitly.
