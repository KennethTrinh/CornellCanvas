There is some hypothesis $h^*$ in the hypothesis space such that every instance in the instance space is mapped to the correct label. A version space is a subset of the hypothesis space. In particular, the version space contains every hypothesis that may still be $h^*$ given our constraints (where our constraints are the instances for which we know the correct label). Try and think of a way to represent instances of the hypothesis space and then use the representation as a way to determine what elements of the hypothesis space are in your version space after factoring in each known instance.
