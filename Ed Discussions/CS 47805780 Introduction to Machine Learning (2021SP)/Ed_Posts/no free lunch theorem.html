I believe A(S) is algorithm A trained on sample set S . Any hypotheses h estimating the target function f could be expressed this way.

The No Free Lunch refers to the fact that ability of an algorithm to express a mapping comes at the "price" of increased risk of overfitting.

A is a learning algorithm, A(S) is the output of that algorithm trained on a set S (i.e. a classifier), and h is a different, more accurate hypothesis.
