The name "Jack in the Box" might be a little misleading. Rather, think of it as an interactive "fun" box. You don't have to have anything pop out, or even a lid. This project is a measure of how well you can integrate actuators with laser-cut components while keeping all of it in an appealing enclosure. Here are a two examples of excellent boxes from last semester: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HUGuXh4PdX0: This student used different gear ratios to make the Totoro figure wiggle around. They had to have precisely mapped this out in Fusion to make everything line up. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qTCcPwRJGcE: This student used several cams to make interesting motions with the birds and flowers. The box also looks stellar. Keep in mind that you aren't limited to the MakerCase boxes that require screws. You can also use finger joints. We suggest using the former as we have the screws and nuts to put the walls together, and you can also take it apart if you need to fix something inside. Hope this helps!
