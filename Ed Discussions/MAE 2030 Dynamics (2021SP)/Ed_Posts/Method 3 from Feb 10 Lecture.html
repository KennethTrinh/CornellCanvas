As to why tanϕ is equal to μ, I had to think of what μ actually is indicating to make sense. μ equals the friction force divided by the normal force right? multiply μ by the normal and you get the friction force. In triangle terms I imagine a triangle made up of the normal force and friction force with the angle ϕ between the normal force and whatever the hypotenuse of that triangle is. tan of any angle is equal to the opposite side length divided by the adjacent side length right? So, if ϕ is the angle opposite the friction force and adjacent to the normal force, tan of ϕ will give us the ratio between those two which is the same as μ. I hope that makes sense and I didn't say anything stupid, apologies in advance if so. This is just how I thought of the problem in an attempt to make sense of it.
