About tonight's prelim 1 starting at 8:30 on Thursday March 4, 2021. We had just enough volunteers, and just enough luck and work by our TAs, to find a smaller better room than Schwartz for the prelim.

Please read this entire post. Much of it duplicates previous posts, but we want to have all things as clear as possible. You have been assigned to one of three categories.

  • Statter Auditorium (room 185), balcony and main level.

  • Hollister 166, the McManus Lounge

  • Online

    • Read your assignment here . If you are on two lists, let us know. if there is any problem, let us know.

    How to scan and submit your exam to Canvas at the end.

  • All students will upload separate .pdf scans for each problem to Canvas immediately after the exam period has ended.

  • There will be an " Assignment" for each problem under "Exams" to use.

  • Staff will review your submissions as they come in, notifying you if scanning errors or file corruption occurs during your upload, so that you may immediately re-upload.

  • You should check your submissions for clarity and completeness after they are uploaded.

  • In-person test takers should bring their smartphone charged and ready to scan. In Stattler, scanning should be done at the front of the room.

  • Online test takers should similarly be ready to scan at the end.

  • Keep your computer Zoom on while you scan with your phone. Direct your computer camera so it can see you doing the scanning. As for the rest of the exam, use the Discussion Zoom room for this.

    • At the exam , both in person and remotely.

  • Closed notes/books etc. No calculators.

  • The exam is designed to be easily completed (if you know the material perfectly) in under 90 minutes. But you will have 90 minutes over time so that there is no time pressure.

  • Bring food and writing equipment. Have those visible, and nothing else (and also your computer if you are taking the prelim online).

  • All books, bags and coats need to be out of sight (under a seat next to you).

  • Look at 2019 exam covers to learn the details of the exam policies.

    • Online camera setup using Discussion Zoom room

  • Monitoring camera. This is our anti-cheating system. You need a camera that has a view of your work environment and your screen. It need not show your face, but it should show your upper body and head, your paper and the desk area around you. That is, your camera view should be such that we could see if you were using your phone or computer or notes of any kind. You could use an external web cam connected to your computer with a long USB cable. Or you could use your phone (with power supply connected), set up with a small tripod or bent coat hanger or whatever.

  • Keep Zoom chat open on your computer so we can make announcements.

  • If possible, check the quality of your setup and camera system at any office hours.

  • The main problems with the setups people have shown me are

  • Camera not far enough back; and

  • Too much clutter on and near desk.

  • See #112 for an example of a good setup.

    • Post questions and comments below.
