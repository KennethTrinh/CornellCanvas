Author: Andy Ruina (admin)

Vote Count: 2

Here are the three possibilities for lecture: 1) 100% online. No in-person component. For this I would use two pieces of paper side-by-side, like I did in lecture 1. I would have no mask. I would probably only see 49 of you at a time. 2) Hybrid with paper. I would lecture in the same room as now, with 9 people present. There would be no fancy camera or camera person, I would work with 2 side-by-side pieces of paper. 3) Hybrid with blackboard. This is what we are trying to do. My sense is that if we can get the microphone to stay on (no dead batteries, no random muting), and we can get the camera to be in sharp mode all of the time, that this would be most liked by everyone. In Spring of 2020 I tried (1) and (3) and people prefer (3). I also prefer (3). And obviously the in person audience prefers (3) to (2). If I am right that (3), well done, is best, I think we have to work on it for a few more lectures. If, on the other hand people feel that there is no hope, then we can go to (1) all online. Hybrid with paper (2) is what I did for all of fall 2020. I think the in-person audience gets little benefit relative to being online. And it's worse for the online people because I am more constrained and awkward. Unless there is clear opinion that, even if it works out well it won't be that good, I would like to keep working on this hybrid thing.
