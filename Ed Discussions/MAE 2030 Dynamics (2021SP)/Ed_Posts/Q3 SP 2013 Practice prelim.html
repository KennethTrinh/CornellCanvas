1) Why does the leftwards mass have negative position values?

The thing here is that the physical model constructed in the MATLAB code, and in the equations, isn't considering collisions between the masses and the wall (or between the masses themselves for that matter), so the displacement of the masses takes place as if the wall wasn't there. As a result, the leftwards mass gets pushed past the wall into the negative position range. It is possible to develop code that accounts for such a collision (MATLAB has a convenient way to do it using " events "), but it's outside the scope of this exam's coverage.

2) Why don't the masses "become more positive and negative at the same time"?

Those initial conditions correspond to both masses being located in exactly the same position x = 1 (again, the model doesn't consider collisions) with no speed. Therefore, the natural behavior is that they will immediately push away from each other in an equal and opposite manner, and perform damped oscillations around their respective equilibrium points until their motion dissipates.
