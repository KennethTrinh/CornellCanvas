I wouldn't say that what I posted was "correct". Rather, it is just one way to estimate the error. The method I used is iterative, so yes, it does cut into the efficiency. There are fancier methods. I just want you to have a sense that you can compare a solution at two different step sizes to estimate the error. This idea works for any method.
