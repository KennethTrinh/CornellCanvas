It's a weird thing, but the units are programmed so that a 1 lbm object has a 1lbf weight on Earth. In metric we have 1 kg m/s^2 = 1 N, but we do not have 1 lbm ft/s^2 = 1 lbf. There is actually a conversion factor between lbm ft/s^2 and lbf, and that factor is g (32.2ft/s^2). We say that 32.2 lbm ft/s^2 = 1 lbf so that the 1 lbm object has a 1 lbf weight on Earth. So, 1 lbm * g / 1 lbf is equal to 1, which is why it is used as a conversion factor here.
