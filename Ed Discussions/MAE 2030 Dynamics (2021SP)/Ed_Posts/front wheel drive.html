Author: Andy Ruina (admin)

Vote Count: 1

One could write a Ph.D. thesis on the relation between between static and dynamic friction. Someone has, in fact. The issue here isn't really about that. It's an issue of sliding or not sliding. When sliding we have, according to Coulomb's law (also called D'avinci's law) $ F= \mu N$ there the force direction is such that it opposes the relative motion. When there is no sliding all that you know is that $|F| \le \mu N$.

Here's how I explained in my section: Because the car is front wheel drive, there is a torque $\tau$ on the front wheel. And because the car is moving at constant speed, there needs a friction $f$ that counteracts the torque. The gravity $G_F$ and the normal force $N_F$ don't provide moments. Similarly, because there is no driving force/torque on the rear wheel, there can't be a friction - otherwise the moment is unbalanced and the wheel will accelerate counterclockwise.
