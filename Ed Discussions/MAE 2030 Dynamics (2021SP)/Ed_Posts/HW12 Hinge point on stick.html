I think the problem statement describes a stick that's only rotating. It doesn't have to be hinged at any point, as long as there is a torque applied to it that keeps it spinning but it's COM is fixed in space. (So if it is hinged then the hinge point has to be the center.)

Write out the expression for the acceleration of the CoM of the stick, you will see that the cross product actually goes to zero for this problem. Eventually, both two terms at the right side of AMB of the stick go to zero, so the hinge can be at any point.
