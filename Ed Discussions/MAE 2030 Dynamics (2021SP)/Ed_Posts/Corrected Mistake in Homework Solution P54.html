For those who don't want to track this down, 17.1.12 is the problem concerning the Javelon. The answer above, paying attention to 4 cases, concerns a rather mathematical and precise answer to the stated problem. But such enumeration is not at the heart of anything you are supposed to be learning in this class. What's the issue? Given the statement of the problem (less than one revolution, javelon tangent to path at landing), there are two ways that the front of the javelon could hit the ground (CW and CCW rotations) and two ways the back could hit the ground. The actual intent was to consider the front hitting the ground with a CW rotation rate. Thus only one of the four cases is really relevant. The thoroughness of the type that Duan Li shows is fine, but not needed, central nor important for what you should be paying attention to. It does reflect careful, literal reading of the problem statement, however! So it is not wrong, it is just, so to speak, tangential :).
