Agree. You don't need to solve ODE for problem 6. But in boarder terms, to solve a 2nd-order ODE, we need 2 boundary conditions. The initial condition $x(0), \dot{x}(0)$ is just one form of the boundary condition. $x(2), \dot{x}(2)$ can also serve as the boundary condition - you just plug $t=2$ in and solve for the unknown constants.
