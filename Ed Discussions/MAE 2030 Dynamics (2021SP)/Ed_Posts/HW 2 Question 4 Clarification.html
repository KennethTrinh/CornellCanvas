Expansion of what Alex wrote above. a. Often you will find homework solutions, or near solutions, here or there, in lecture, recitation, the book, or on the www. At least a fraction of your homework is not meant as a puzzle or challenge. It is, instead, something that you should learn to do. So, although you could, in this case, copy from lecture, and then add comments, you should instead learn to write the code, from beginning to end without looking up anything , anywhere (with the possible exception of looking up the syntax of this or that command). b. Exponential growth ODE : $\dot x = \phantom{-}bx$, with $b>0$. Exponential decay ODE : $\dot x = -bx$, with $b>0$. Sines and cosines : $\ddot x = -cx$, with $c>0$. Growth & decay simultaneously: $\ddot x = \phantom{-}cx$, with $c>0$.
