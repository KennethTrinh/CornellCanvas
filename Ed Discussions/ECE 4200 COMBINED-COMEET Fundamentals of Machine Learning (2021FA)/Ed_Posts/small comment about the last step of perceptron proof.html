in the end of the proof of perceptron, we noted that after the perceptron makes j mistakes:

\frac{\left({j}\gamma\right)^2}{\left(2j\gamma\right)\left(2\right)}\le1

must hold (by using observations 2,3, and 4) . This implies that

j\le\frac{4}{\gamma^2}

thus implying that the number of mistakes is at most

\frac{4}{\gamma^2}
