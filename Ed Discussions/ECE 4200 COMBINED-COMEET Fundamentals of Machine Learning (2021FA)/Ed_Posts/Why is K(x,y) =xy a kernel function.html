So, the definition of a kernel is that

K\left(X,Y\right)=\ \Phi\left(X\right)\cdot\Phi\left(Y\right)

for a Phi. Here we have Phi(X)=X then we are done.

If x is +ve and y is -ve, then K(x,y) is negative. The kernel can take negative values. That does not violate the definition of kernels.

1. Phi(x)=x is the identity function, so for all x, Phi(x)=x. That means that Phi(y)=y for all y. You must have the same Phi for both arguments of K.
