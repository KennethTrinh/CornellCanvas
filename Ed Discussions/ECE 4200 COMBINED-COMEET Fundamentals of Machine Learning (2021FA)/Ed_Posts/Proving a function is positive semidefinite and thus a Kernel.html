We actually proved part 1 in the class, so you can copy that part here. Recall the definition of Kernel function: If there exists a Phi:R^d->R^m such that K\left(X,Y\right)=\Phi\left(X\right)\cdot\Phi\left(Y\right) for all X,Y then K is a kernel function. For parts 2, 3 you need to argue whether you can find such a Phi or not? Please go through those parts of the lecture including the examples where we showed somethings are not a kernel function and somethings are.
