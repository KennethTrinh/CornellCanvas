Author: David Delchamps (admin)

Vote Count: 1

There is one. If $y ( n ) = e ^ {j n \omega _ o} x ( n )$, then $\widehat{Y} ( \omega ) = \widehat{X} ( \omega - \omega _ o )$.

------------------------------------

Author: Ariana Haghighi (student)

Does it apply to n-pt DFTs?

------------------------------------

Author: David Delchamps (admin)

Vote Count: 1

No, because you can't make sense of $\widehat{X} ( \omega - \omega _ o )$ when $\widehat{X} _ k$ is just a list of $N$ numbers indexed by $k$.
