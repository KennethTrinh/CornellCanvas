For the symmetry properties, why is X(w) an even function when x(t) is an even function? I try to use change of variable to prove this, but what I get is that X(w) should be an odd function when x(t) is even.

$\widehat{X} ( - \Omega ) = \int x ( t ) e ^ {j \Omega t} d t = \int x ( - \tau ) e ^ {- j \Omega \tau} d \tau = \int x ( t ) e ^ {- j \Omega \tau} d \tau = \widehat{X} ( \Omega)$. The second equality follows from the change of variable $\tau = - t$. Remember that $d \tau = - d t$ and $\int _ {- \infty} ^ \infty d t = \int _ {\infty} ^ {- \infty} d \tau = - \int _ {- \infty} ^ \infty d \tau$.
