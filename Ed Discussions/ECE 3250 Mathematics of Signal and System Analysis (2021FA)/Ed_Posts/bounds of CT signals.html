You've mixed discrete- and continuous-time notation here.

In discrete time, you need $x ( n ) \rightarrpw 0$ as $n \rightarrow \pm \infty$ for $x$ to be absolutely summable.

In continuous time, you don't need $x ( t ) \rightarrow 0$ as $ t \rightarrow \pm \infty$ for $x$ to be absolutely integrable. The counterexamples are kind of weird, but even decent counterexamples exist.

And you can have $x ( n ) \rightarrow 0$ as $n \rightarrow \pm \infty$ even for non-summable signals, e.g. $x ( n ) = ( 1 / n ) u ( n - 1 )$. Similarly in continuous time.
