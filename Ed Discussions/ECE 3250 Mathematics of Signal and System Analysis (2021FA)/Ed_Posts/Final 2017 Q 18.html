Author: David Delchamps (admin)

Vote Count: 3

It should be $X ( z ) = \frac{z}{z - 5}$ with ROC $0 < | z | < 5$, and indeed $Y ( z ) = H ( z ) X ( z )$ and the ROC for that is the intersection of those of $X ( z )$ and $H ( z )$, namely $0.3 < | z | < 5$. In (b), $x$ has no $z$-transform --- it's a constant input signal --- specifically, $x ( n ) = 7 ( 1 ^ n )$ for all $n$, and since $1$ is in the ROC for $H ( z )$, we have $y _ 2 ( n ) = 7 H ( 1 ) ( 1 ^ n ) = 7 H ( 1 )$. Many people opted to compute the convolution of $h$ with $x$ instead, which is also okay but more time-consuming.
