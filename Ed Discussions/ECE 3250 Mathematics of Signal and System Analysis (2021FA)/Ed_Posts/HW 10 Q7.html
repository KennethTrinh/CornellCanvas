Author: David Delchamps (admin)

Vote Count: 0

Turns out that $y _ 1$, the ordinary convolution of $x _ 1$ and $x _ 2$ viewed as $N$-point signals, is the same as the circular convolution of those two $N$-point signals. Thus you get $y _ 1$ by taking the inverse $N$-point DFT of

$\left ( \widehat{Y} _ 1 \right ) _ k = \left ( \widehat{X} _ 1 \right ) _ k \left ( \widehat{X} _ 2 \right ) _ k$, $0 \leq k < N$.
