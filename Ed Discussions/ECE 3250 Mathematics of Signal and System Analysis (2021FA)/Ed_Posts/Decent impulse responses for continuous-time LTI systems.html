The general form of the impulse response of a continuous-time system (for us) is a decent signal plus possibly a few impulses --- see Standing Assumption at bottom of page 113 in the monograph. Furthermore, BIBO stability demands only that the "decent part" of the system's impulse response be an $L ^ 1$-signal.
