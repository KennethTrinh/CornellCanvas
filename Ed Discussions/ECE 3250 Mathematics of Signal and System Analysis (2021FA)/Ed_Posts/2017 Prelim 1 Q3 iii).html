We know that <k^3> mod a is equivalent to k^3, and that <k^(a+2)> mod a is equivalent to <k^(a-1+3)> mod a. If you multiply both sides by k^-3, you get <k^(a-1)> mod a is equivalent to 1, which using Fermat's Little Theorem should be true.
