You're supposed to look at it and know it's an averager. You can do the $p _ a$ thing, as well, and you'll get essentially the same thing, as follows: $\lim _ {a \rightarrow 0} \frac{1}{a} \int _ {t - 14} ^ t p _ a ( \tau ) d \tau = \left \{ \begin{array}{cl} 1 & {\rm whem} \; t - 14 < 0 < t \\ 0 & {\rm when} \; 0 < t - 14 \; {\rm or} \; t < 0 \end{array} \right .$ and it comes out to $1 / 2$ when $t = 0$ or $t - 14 = 0$.
