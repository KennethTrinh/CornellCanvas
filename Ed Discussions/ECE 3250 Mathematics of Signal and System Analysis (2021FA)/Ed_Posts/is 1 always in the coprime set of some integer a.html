Given some positive integer a, we have defined in class that Z_a^{\cdot}\ is\ the\ set\ of\ all\ integers\ from\ 1\ to\ a\ -1\ that\ are\ coprime\ with\ a. So, is 1 considered in this set. In other words, is 1 coprime with a?

Author: David Delchamps (admin)

Vote Count: 0

Absolutely. Coprime means "no common divisors other than 1." And 1 fits that bill with any $a$.
