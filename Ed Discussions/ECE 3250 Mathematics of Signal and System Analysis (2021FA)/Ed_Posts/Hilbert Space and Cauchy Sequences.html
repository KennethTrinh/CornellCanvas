We met Cauchy sequences talking about rationals and reals. There are Cauchy sequences of rational numbers that don't converge to a rational number ---- if you think of the rational numbers as "all available numbers," those Cauchy sequences just don't converge. --- the set of rational numbers isn't big enough to contain limits for all its Cauchy sequences. On the other hand, every Cauchy sequence of real numbers converges to a real number, so the set of real numbers is big enough in that way. Think of an inner product space $V$ with non-converging Cauchy sequences as kind of like the rational numbers -- it's not big enough to have limits for all its Cauchy sequnces. Think of a Hilbert space as kind of like the real numbers --- it's big enough to have limits for all its Cauchy sequences.
