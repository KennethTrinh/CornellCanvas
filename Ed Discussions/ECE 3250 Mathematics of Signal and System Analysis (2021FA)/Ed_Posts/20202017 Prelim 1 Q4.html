To your first question: $p$ is coprimbe with every integer below it except 0.

To the second, for $p$ to divide that product, it has to be 2 or 7 by uniqueness of prime factorizations. Since $p$ is odd, it hast to be 7.

The only prime divisors of a product of prime powers are the primes appearing in the product. This follows from the old if $p | a b$ then $p | a$ or $p | b$ or both thing.
