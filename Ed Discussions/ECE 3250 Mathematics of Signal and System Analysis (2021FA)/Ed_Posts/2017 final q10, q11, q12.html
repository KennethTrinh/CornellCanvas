10(ii) The system's I/O mapping is in the general form $S ( x ) = h _ 0 * x +$ shift term, where $h _ 0$ is a decent signal. Any I/O mapping in that form is LTI. BTW your formula isn't quite right --- you need to shift the $x$ under the integral rather than the limit on the integral. Your approach to (iv) is fine. You could also just plug in $e ^ {j \Omega t}$ for $x ( t )$ and you'd discover that the integral doesn't converge, so the system doesn't have a frequency response. The shift (i.e. $d$-type) terms in $h$ never affect existence of frequency response --- it's all about $h _ 0$. The fundamental frequency is $7$ radians per second. The fundamental period is $2 \pi / 7$ sec. $\widehat{X} ( 3 \times 2 \pi / 5 )$ equals what you get when you plug $k = 3$ into the expression in the problem statement. It'll be the sum of four terms.
