Why is lecture do you often say "with specification" instead of just "equals"?

----------- REPLIES -----------

Author: David Delchamps (admin)

Vote Count: 0

The specification of a signal $x$ is just a description of what $x ( n )$ is for every $n \in \mathbb{Z}$. This sentence feels awkward to me: let $x$ be the signal that equals $x ( n ) = \left \{ \begin{array}{cl} 3 & {\rm when} \; n \geq 7 \\ 0 & {\rm when} \; n < 7 . \end{array} \right .$ It feels better for me to put "with specification" there instead of "equals." Like, the equal sign does the "equals" part.
