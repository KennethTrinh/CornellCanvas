Just (iii) and (iv) are true. The Fourier series would need to have finitely many nonzero terms for (i) to be true. Real-valued means $x ( t )$ is a real number for all $t$. Fot this signal, it's true because the $\pm k$ terms pair up via Euler to give cosines and their imaginary parts cancel out. Specifically,

$x ( t ) = 1 / 7 + \sum _ {k = 1} ^ \infty \frac{2}{k ^ 2 + 7} \cos ( k ( 2 \pi / 17 ) t )$ for all $t$.
