You're not missing anything. Take the inner product of the given expression with $w _ 1$ and you get

$c _ 1 \langle w _ 1 , w _ 1 \rangle + c _ 2 \langle w _ 2 , w _ 1 \rangle + \cdots + c _ n \langle w _ n , w _ 1 \rangle = 0$.

The first term in the sum is $c _ 1$ and the rest are zero because the $w _ k$ are orthonormal. Conclusion: $c _ 1 = 0$.

Next, take the inner product with $w _ 2$, and you'll discover that $c _ 2 = 0$.

Similarly you find that $c _ k = 0$ for all $k$, Thus: if you have a linear combo of that form that equals zero, all the coefficients must be zero --- so the $w _ k$ are linearly independent.
