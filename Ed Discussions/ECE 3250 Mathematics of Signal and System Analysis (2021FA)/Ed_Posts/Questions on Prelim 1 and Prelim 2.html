Sorry it took me so long to get to this post.

3(iii) They both have "multiplicative inverses" in $\mathbb{Z} _ a ^ *$ so their product mod $a$ can't be zero. Like, multiply by the multiplicative inverse of $m$ and you get $\langle n \rangle _ a = 0$, which is impossible for any $n \in \mathbb{Z} _ a$.

5(i) When $A$ is $[ 0 , 1 )$, $\min ( A ) = 0$ but $A$ has no max.

A convergent sequence need not converge "monotonically" to its limit. Like, one term could be an outlier. E..g this sequence converges to 0: $5, 4, 3, 10^{59}, 2, 1 , 0, 0 , 0 , 0 , 0 \ldots$

3(ii) A linear combo will preserve having 19 as a period, but won't necessarily preserve having 19 as fundamental period.

4(iv) just says that the $k = 23$ Fourier series coefficient of $x$ is zero, which it is because $x$ has nonzero Fourier coefficients only out as far as $k = \pm 19$.
