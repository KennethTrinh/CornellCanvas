Since p is prime and larger than 2 we know that p-1 must be even, or it would be divisible by 2. This tells us that the set Z*_(p-1) can't contain any even number that is a multiple of 2 because it is the set of all co-primes with p-1 . Therefore, e and d must both be odd. EXAMPLE: p=5 (a prime number) p-1=4 (even) Z_(p-1) = {0,1,2,3} Z*_(p-1) = {1,3}
