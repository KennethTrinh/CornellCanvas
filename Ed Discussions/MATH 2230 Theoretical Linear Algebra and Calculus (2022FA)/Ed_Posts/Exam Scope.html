Author: Reyer Sjamaar (admin)

Vote Count: 1

Sometimes the following method works. Suppose you've found a constrained critical point $\mathbf{a}\in M$ of a function $f$ using Lagrange multipliers or whatever. Suppose you are able to find a parametrization $\gamma$ of $M$, or of a portion of $M$ that contains $\mathbf{a}$. Then the nature of the constrained critical point $\mathbf{a}$ can be determined from the unconstrained Hessian of the function $f\circ\gamma$.

Yes, the "portion of $M$" has to be of the form $M\cap V$, where $V$ is an open set in $\R^n$ that contains $\mathbf{a}$.
