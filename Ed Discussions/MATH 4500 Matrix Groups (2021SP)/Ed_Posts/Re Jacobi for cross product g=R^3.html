The vector $(x\cdot z)y-(x\cdot y)z$ is a linear combination of $y$ and $z$ with coefficients $x\cdot z\in\mathbf{R}$ and $x\cdot y\in\mathbf{R}$. The identity $x\times(y\times z)=(x\cdot z)y-(x\cdot y)z$ for vectors $x$, $y$, $z\in\mathbf{R}^3$ is a standard identity in vector calculus. It can be verified by straightforward algebra. If we add the identities $x\times(y\times z)=(x\cdot z)y-(x\cdot y)z$ $z\times(x\times y)=(z\cdot y)x-(z\cdot x)y$ $y\times(z\times x)=(y\cdot x)-(y\cdot z)x$ we get $x\times(y\times z)+z\times(x\times y)+y\times(z\times x)=0,$ i.e. the Jacobi identity.
