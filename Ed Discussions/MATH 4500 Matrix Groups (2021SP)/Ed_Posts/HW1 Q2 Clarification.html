I'm a little confused by the wording for the definition of "center" in question 2. Question 2 says: "The center of a group G is the subset Z(G) consisting of all g in G with the property that gh=hg for all h in G. " Could someone clarify this? Thanks!

If $g$ and $h$ are elements of a group $G$, we say that $g$ commute s with $h$ if $gh=hg$. So an element is in the center of $G$ if and only if it commutes with every element of $G$. If $G$ is commutative, then every element commutes with every other, so $Z(G)=G$. But if $G$ is not commutative, the center can be very much smaller than $G$.
