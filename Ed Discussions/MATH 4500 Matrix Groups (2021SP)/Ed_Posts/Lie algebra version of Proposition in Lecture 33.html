Yes, the added condition that $\gamma$ be a Lie algebra homomorphism is there to ensure that the fixed-point space $\mathfrak{g}_0=\{\,x\in\mathfrak{g}\mid\gamma(x)=x\,\}$ is a Lie subalgebra, i.e. a real linear subspace closed under the bracket. This is shown as follows. Since $\gamma$ is real linear, the set $\mathfrak{g}_0$, being the eigenspace of $\gamma$ for the eigenvalue $1$, is a real linear subspace of $\mathfrak{g}$. Moreover, if $x$, $y\in\mathfrak{g}_0$, then $\gamma(x)=x$ and $\gamma(y)=y$, so $\gamma([x,y])=[\gamma(x),\gamma(y)]=[x,y].$ Therefore $[x,y]$ is in the fixed-point space of $\gamma$, i.e. $[x,y]\in\mathfrak{g}_0$.
