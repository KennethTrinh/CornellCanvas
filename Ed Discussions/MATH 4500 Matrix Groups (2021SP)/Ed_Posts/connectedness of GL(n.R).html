Yes, the determinant $\det(A)$ of a complex matrix $A$ depends continuously on $A$. However, the determinant of a complex matrix is a complex number. In the complex plane there are paths from $1$ to $-1$ which avoid the origin.

Here is an explicit path of complex invertible matrices from $I$ to $J$:

$$\gamma(t)=\begin{pmatrix}\exp(\pi it/2)&&&&\\&1&&&\\&&1&&\\&&&\ddots&\\&&&&1\end{pmatrix}.$$

We have $\gamma(0)=I$, $\gamma(1)=J$, and $\det(\gamma(t))=\exp(\pi it/2)\ne0$ for all $t$.
