Yes. Yes. There is an inner product on the algebra of quaternions for which the associated norm is $|x|=\sqrt{x^*x}$. $M(n,\mathbf{F})$ is not a group, but a vector space (in fact an algebra) over the field $\mathbf{F}$. The entries of the matrices in $M(n,\mathbf{F})$ are in $\mathbf{F}$. We can scalar multiply such matrices by scalars from $\mathbf{F}$. No, $y$ is an arbitrary quaternion.
