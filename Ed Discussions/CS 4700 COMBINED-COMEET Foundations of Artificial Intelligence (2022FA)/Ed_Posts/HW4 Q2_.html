Author: Laura Greenstreet (admin)

Vote Count: 0

A single resolution step has the form: $$(\alpha \lor \gamma) \land (\beta \lor \neg \gamma) \Rightarrow (\alpha \lor \beta)$$ For example, an initial resolution step in our question could be $$ (p_1) \land (\neg p_1 \land p_2) \Rightarrow (p_2)$$ Resolution is a type of proof by contradiction. So, in our question we are trying to show $(\text{KB} \land \neg p_n)$ is inconsistent. If you reach an empty clause, you have shown there are no model consistent with $\text{KB} \land \neg p_n$ and thus $p_n$ must be true. Let me know if that clears things up or if you have other questions.
