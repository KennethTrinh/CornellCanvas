To hopefully avoid confusion as there are a couple of different versions of the formula shown on that page, we'll be flexible about the exact form of your answer but try to get something in base 2, e, or 10. Ex. if you want to approximate $k!$ you can use the approximation $ \text{ln} k! \approx \text{ln} k - k$ and then translate it to base $e$ by plugging the approximation into $k! = e^{\text{ln} k!}$.
