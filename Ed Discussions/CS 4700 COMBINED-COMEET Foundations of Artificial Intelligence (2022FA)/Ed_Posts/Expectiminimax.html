Author: Laura Greenstreet (admin)

Consider investing $1000 where one investment has a guaranteed return of $1100 while another option has a 90% chance of losing all your money and a 10% chance of of giving you $11,001. The first option has an expectation of $1100 while the second option has an expectation of $1100.10. So, if you are maximizing expectation, you should choose the second option. However, in many cases you would prefer the guaranteed return over a high-risk investment.

Author: Laura Greenstreet (admin)

Expectminimax plays optimally in expectation. It is just in some cases we may be willing to trade a reduction in expectation for less variability, because the reward from a single run may differ significantly from the expectation. Ex. with the second investment 9 times out of 10 you'll lose all of your money even though in expectation you get $1100.10.
