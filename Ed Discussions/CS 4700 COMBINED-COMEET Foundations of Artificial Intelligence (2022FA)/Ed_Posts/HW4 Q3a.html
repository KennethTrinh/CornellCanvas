Author: Laura Greenstreet (admin)

The minimal unsatisfiable core should be a subset of the original clauses such that the subset is unsatisfiable but removing any clause from the subset makes the set satisfiable. Neither $P_{11} \lor P_{12}$ nor $P_{1,1}$ cannot be in the unsatisfiable core, because they are not part of the original set of clauses.

Author: Laura Greenstreet (admin)

Vote Count: 0

They are not from either group. Clauses in group I have the form $(P_{i1} \lor P_{i2} \lor ... \lor P_{iN})$ for pigeon $i$ and clauses in group II have the form $(\neg P_{i_1j} \lor \neg P_{i_2 j})$ for pigeons $i_1, i_2$ and pigeonhole $j$. An example of a valid subset is just the clauses from group I.
