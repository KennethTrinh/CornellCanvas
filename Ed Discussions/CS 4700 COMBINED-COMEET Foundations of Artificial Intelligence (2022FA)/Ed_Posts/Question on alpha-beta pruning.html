I am a bit confused about the definition of "ancestors" in the slides: A MIN node can be $\alpha$ pruned if its own $\beta$ satisfies $\beta \leq \alpha$ of some MAX ancestor nodes. Here by ancestors, do they refer to MAX nodes that have been explored so far? Or they mean MAX nodes that have been explored and are "directly" above the current node. For example, in HW3 1c, node M could be pruned if we consider node G as its ancestor. We have explored node G but it is in a different branch. Thanks!

An ancestor node must be on the path from the root to the node. I.e. in HW3 1c, the ancestors of node M are F, C, A. Nodes in other branches will influence whether or not you evaluate a node, but that is captured in the $\alpha/\beta$ values of the ancestor nodes.
