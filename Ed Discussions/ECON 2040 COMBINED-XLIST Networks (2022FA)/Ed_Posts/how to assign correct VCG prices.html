VCG prices are dependent on the harm that is done to the other buyers in the market by taking an item. Let's consider buyer x, who gets item A. y gets item B which they value at 10 and w gets item C which they value at 4. z gets nothing, which they value at 0. This matching is maximizes total valuation. If x takes item A, then the valuation of the rest of the buyers in the market is (10 + 4 + 0) = 14. To calculate harm, we consider their valuation when x is not in the market. If this were the case, then buyer y would take item A, which they value at 15. Buyer w would get item B, which they value at 8. Buyer z would get item C, which they value at 2. Thus, the total valuation in a market without buyer x is (15 + 8 + 2) = 25. The difference between these two valuations is the harm that x does by entering the market and taking item A. This gives us 25 - 14 = 11 as the harm done. Thus, our VCG price for buyer x getting item A is 11. The remaining prices can be determined in much the same way.
