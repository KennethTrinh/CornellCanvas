I was looking through the textbook to get some clarity on how to approach Q4b and Q4c in pset5 and was confused about two things:

1) how can we check the equilibrium condition for z in this example? Is it simply the fact that z's payoff will reduce if z increases or decreases their bid?

2) Why do we not confirm if y's payoff will increase if y bids below z's current bid?

For reference this example is on pg 454 of the pdf and references Fig 15.6.

1) Z's payoff only changes when Z increases or decreases their bid such that they get a different item (as for a given item, the price isn't dependent on one's bid). Here, it's only relevant that Z could increase their bid. However, we would see that they would have to bid above 4 to get an item that would only give them a revenue of (4*1)=4, meaning that their payoff of 0 would remain the same and they have no incentive to change. To check that a given buyer is in equilibrium, we would verify that they have no incentive (cannot increase their payoff) from changing their bid

2) We should check it, but it's fairly trivial, which is why I think the book skipped it. If y bids below z's bid, then they get an item (Item C) that they have 0 value for. This means that their payoff (value-price) cannot be above 0, and since they have a positive payoff where they are, they would have no incentive to change to get this item
