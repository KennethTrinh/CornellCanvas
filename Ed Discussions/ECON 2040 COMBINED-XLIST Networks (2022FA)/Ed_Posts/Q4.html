Author: David Cai (admin)

Vote Count: 1

STC is either satisfied or violated. Two conditions have to be met in order for a student to violate STC. [1] The student has at least 2 strong ties. [2] The student has at least one pair of strong ties with other students despite those other two students not having any tie with each other. Even though a student might have 19 strong ties with other students on a floor, if there's one instance in which the student has ties that would violate STC, such as with other students on different floors, then they can no longer satisfy STC.

Author: David Cai (admin)

Any node that meets the two conditions I stated above would violate STC. Any node that doesn't meet the two conditions above would satisfy STC. For question 3C, if all but one node meet the two conditions above, then all but one node would violate STC, and hence, only one node would satisfy STC. This explanation is not to confirm whether your answer is correct or not, just to explain a way to approach this.
