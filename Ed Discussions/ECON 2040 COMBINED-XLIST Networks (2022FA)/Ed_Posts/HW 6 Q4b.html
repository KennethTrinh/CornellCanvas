I recommend not considering the probability of the entire R-Cascade occurring, but instead considering the probability of the elements that cause the R-Cascade to happen. In essence, you should be able to find the event B (which is hinted at by part a) such that because B occurred, we ended up in an R-Cascade (what event happened that caused us to be in this incorrect cascade?). You can then find Pr[B|G], which should hopefully be easier - hope this helps!
