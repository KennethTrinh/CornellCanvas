Author: Caleb Chin (admin)

A strategy does not have to be beat by the same strategy in every case to be strictly dominated. Like you said, as long as every payoff for this strategy is dominated by some other payoff for another strategy, it is strictly dominated (given that the other payoffs are strictly greater and not equal). EDIT: the answer above is incorrect. If a strategy is strictly dominated by another strategy, then it is the case that it has to be beaten by the same strategy in every case. For more clarification, see Jason's comment below or section 6.10 part B in the textbook.
