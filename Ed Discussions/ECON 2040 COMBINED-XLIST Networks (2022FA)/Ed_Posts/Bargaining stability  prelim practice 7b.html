A stable outcome is when, for all edges, the values that the nodes at the end of the edge receive is less than the value of the edge. This is because it means that the edge isn't used, but the nodes collectively would get more by doing a deal along it (as they would split a total value more than their current total value).

For the given scenario, C and D are currently earning $8 in the setup. Because C and D have a collective value higher than that of an edge, doing a deal along C-D instead means that they would collectively earn less, so neither would want to do it (in order to convince the other, they'd have to adopt a deal to get <4, which is less than they earn currently, so they would not)

Author: Aidan McNay (student)

Yes - Nash Bargaining outcomes are a subset of stable outcomes (there can be multiple stable outcomes, but only one Nash Bargaining). Stable is when there is a positive surplus, and Nash Bargaining is when said surplus is split equally
