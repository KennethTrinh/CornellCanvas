"Second equilibrium" and z'' don't generalize well if there's more equilibria than we've seen previously. However, I think your intuition is correct; if reservation price > actual price, then the consumers around our current z want to buy as well, meaning that over time, the number of consumers that buy will increase (naturally, until this relation becomes false, which would occur at an equilibria)
