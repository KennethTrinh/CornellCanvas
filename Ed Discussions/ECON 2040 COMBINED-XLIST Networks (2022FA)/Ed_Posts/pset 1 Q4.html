Here's an example. Let's suppose that there are 3 students on floor 1 and 3 students on floor 2. Suppose that students that are on the same floor have strong ties between them, and that students on different floors have weak ties between them. Then the above graph visualizes these relationships (the triangle on the left is floor 1, and the triangle on the right is floor 2), with bold lines being strong ties and thin lines being weak ties. We can then check if a node satisfies strong triadic closure by examining which nodes it shares strong ties with. In the problem, where there are 20 students, it will be hard to draw the network out, but you can still think about the types of ties each individual student has. Hope this helps!
