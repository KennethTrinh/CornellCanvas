c) Yes - it is because the probability of a good state is greater for a high signal, and a bad state is higher for a low signal

d) Yes (we determine that you go with your own signal in the event of equal probability, which can be calculated with Bayes' Rule, as we need to factor in the fact that the previous person got a high signal)

d) Yup (looking at the conditional probability with Bayes Rule, we can see that in either case (high or low signal), a good state is still more likely)
