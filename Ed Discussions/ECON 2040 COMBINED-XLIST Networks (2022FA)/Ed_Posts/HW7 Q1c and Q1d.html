I don't believe either of these cases are correct (certainly not to the closest equilibria, and I'm not sure what you mean by "upper and lower bound")

When looking at an operating point (a value of z), consider consumer x, where x=z. More importantly, consider the consumers directly greater or less than them (for larger or smaller values of x). If z were to shift (slightly) in that direction, would those consumers buy the product? (Remember that if a consumer's reservation price is above the price, they will buy, and if it is below, they will not)

Let's consider the case when we analyze a small shift to the right of z. If those new consumers (greater value of x) would buy, then the shift would continue, until we get to a point where the new consumers that would result from the shift wouldn't buy. If these new consumers instead wouldn't buy the product, then z would decrease and shift back to where it was previously
