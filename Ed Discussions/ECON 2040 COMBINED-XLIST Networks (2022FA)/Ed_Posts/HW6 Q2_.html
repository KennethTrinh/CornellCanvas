It is just an application of what we have already learned. First consider the student's guess with no marble draw - what range of p would cause them to guess MB? There should be some cutoff value of p where what they should guess switches. You can consider this as that the probability of the urn being MB is a function of p (albeit a very simple one), and you have some range of probability that the urn is MB for which the student should guess MB (corresponding to a range of p) From here, we can approach the problem with them drawing a marble. From this, one can calculate the probability that the urn is MB. The student will logically guess MB for a range of these probabilities (the same as above), which corresponds to a range of p (as the probability that the urn is MB depends on p). Hope this helps!
