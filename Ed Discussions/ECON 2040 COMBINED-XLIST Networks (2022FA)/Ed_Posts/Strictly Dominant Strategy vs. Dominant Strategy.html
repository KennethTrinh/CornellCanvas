Just to clarify, we've defined strictly dominant as all the payoffs being strictly greater than payoffs from any other strategy (i.e. only >). In this case, dominant strategies can be defined by payoffs "greater than or equal to" payoffs from any other strategy. See section 6.3 (around pdf pg 9) in the textbook: https://www.cs.cornell.edu/home/kleinber/networks-book/networks-book-ch06.pdf
