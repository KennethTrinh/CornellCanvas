You're correct that there is now a new path A->E->B which gets you to the final destination. The incorporation of the (two-way) E-C edge of 0 hours of travel provides a new path for those who are currently at E (or C) and might want to switch paths in case it's faster. Now, instead of 3 "two-edge-long" paths from A to B, there is a possibility to take another route halfway through.
