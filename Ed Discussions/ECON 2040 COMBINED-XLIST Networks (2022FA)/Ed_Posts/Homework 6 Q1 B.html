Not quite - if you did so, you would be cubing all of the components of Bayes' Rule, some of which are true in general regardless of the time frame (and therefore shouldn't be cubed). Instead, can you think of which components might differ from one day versus three days? How would you take your answer for one day, and use it for three consecutive days?
