It's almost never the case that there exists an equilibrium where only one player randomizes. Even if that one person randomizes, it will be because the payoffs are equal for both strategy, thus resulting in a 50/50 chance.

It's possible to construct payoff matrices where one player randomizes and the other does not, but the situations are so rare that I doubt we will ask you that type of question.
