Assuming you are referring to HW 3, this grading structure was to avoid over-penalizing if you used incorrect calculations or interpretations from 4a that continued in 4b. Since 4a is a 4-point problem, a correct x , y , z , and a reasonable attempt are each worth one point. (It sounds like you got the point for z and the reasonable attempt, 2/4).
