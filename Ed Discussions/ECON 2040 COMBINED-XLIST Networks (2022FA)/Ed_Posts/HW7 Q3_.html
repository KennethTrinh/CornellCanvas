1) c represents the constant offset. We know that f(k) should decrease proportional to 1/k^a , but what is it decreasing from? If we just had f(k)=1/k^a , then we'd always have fractional pages getting the number of views, which isn't realistic. c therefore represents some constant coefficient that helps scale our model to real-life (or, if it's easier to think about, it's how many pages get one view, which can be verified from the formula)

2) The intuition is only that they are governed by different power laws (if at all). We know that the power law will be something of the form b/k^a , but those exact coefficients depend on the application/exact source of data (see lecture slides from Nov. 2nd for some examples of a ). Some intuition for why we might perform this check might be to see whether the pieces of data came from the same phenomena (governed by the same law), or if they are, whether that phenomena itself is governed by a power law at all
