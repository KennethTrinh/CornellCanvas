I'm not sure if I understand your question: what do you mean by "adjusting one bidder's price increases other bidder's price?" Advertisers can only change their own bid (which may be the price that you're referring to?). Do you mean increasing/decreasing their payoffs under different adjustments of bids? If this is what you meant, then: it would not be a nash equilibrium if the advertiser adjust their bid and can get higher payoffs with their adjusted bid, because the advertisers have incentive to change.
