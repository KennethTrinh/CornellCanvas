Not quite:

  • Density measures fraction of connections nodes in a cluster have going to nodes outside the cluster

  • Interconnectedness measures the fraction of connections nodes in a set have going to other nodes inside a set

    • Therefore, if a node has a higher fraction of connections to nodes in the cluster, it must have a lower fraction going to nodes outside the cluster, and vice versa. In this way, there is a tradeoff between interconnectedness and density of the set at large

    (Since interconnectedness isn't well defined for this, I took it as a fraction. One could also consider it as the raw number of connections to other nodes in the set, in which case they wouldn't be as related, although still somewhat. You could have a low density cluster with high or low interconnectedness)
