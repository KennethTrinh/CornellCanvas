If you wish, there are two examples on the slides from 11/16 that use different numbers. The general idea is that a strategy is ESS if, when a large majority of others adopt that strategy (a fraction 1-x for small x) and a small number adopt the other, the given strategy is the best response. You can just calculate the payoffs using those percentages, and come to conclusions by comparing your outcomes (remembering that x is small but still exists)
