Citing the problem in Chapter 22.6, if the price is equal to the value, then the seller will sell (the "real-life" intuition is that with a payoff of 0, the seller could go either way, so if there's a buyer that's pressuring them to sell at that price, they would probably do so.) As an aside, I hesitate to use the word "profit", as that could be worked into their payoff - ex. they need x amount of profit on a purchase to stay afloat as a business/pay their workers and earn 0 payoff overall. I think payoff in general provides a cleaner description of one's motivation - they will do something if they get a positive payoff (as well as a seller's "value" isn't well-defined; cost to make? What they need to pay their workers in addition?)
