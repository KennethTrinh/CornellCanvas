Author: Kenny Peng (admin)

Vote Count: 0

You should think of a Nash equilibrium as a property of both the strategies of player A and player B (neither player would change what they are doing given the other player's strategy). Therefore, if any of the probabilities for a mixed strategy you find are negative, then it isn't valid.
