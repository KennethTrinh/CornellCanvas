Terminology clarification: a node has neighbors, whereas a cluster (of nodes) has density. A cluster is considered to have density n if all nodes in the cluster have at least a fraction n of their neighbors also in the cluster. Clusters are similar to a set (of nodes), but we consider them clusters when talking about their density.
