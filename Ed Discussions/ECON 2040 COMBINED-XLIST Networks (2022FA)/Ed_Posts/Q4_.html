Author: Kenny Peng (admin)

Vote Count: 0

When we say "there is a strong tie between each student on the first floor and each student on the second floor," we mean that every student on the first floor shares a strong tie with every student on the second floor (and vice versa). When we say that "there is a strong tie between each pair of people who live on the same floor," we mean that all students on a single floor share a strong tie with all other students on that floor. (When we talk about "pairs" of people, we don't mean roommates, but rather any two people on the floor.)
