It will die down to .6, assuming we have a decreasing r(x) with x (like 1-x) and increasing f(z) with z (like 4z). This is because the person z will not buy the product at the current price if he believes z fraction are using it, because f(z)*r(z)<p. In order to compensate for this, the guess has to decrease until it reaches a self fulfilling equilibrium.

Author: Aidan McNay (student)

Nope - also holds for any z > 0.4. If z's in (0.4,0.6), then person z and their near neighbors will want to buy the product( f(z)r(z)>p ), causing usage to increase. Conversely, if z > 0.6, person z will not want to buy the product ( f(z)r(z)<p ), causing usage to decrease. This naturally converges to the equilibrium at z=0.6
