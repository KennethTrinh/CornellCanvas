To my understanding, any market-clearing prices will result in social welfare maximizing: "Optimality of Market-Clearing Prices: For any set of market-clearing prices, a perfect matching in the resulting preferred-seller graph has the maximum total valuation of any assignment of sellers to buyers." the maximum total valuation here is the same thing as social-welfare maximizing. It doesn't really matter if the market clearing prices are the minimum, and the reason is that when calculating the social-welfare, we are adding the sum of prices and the sum of net value for buyers. Imagine prices go up, net value goes down, but overall if it's market clearing, it's still the same social welfare. I hope this helps!
