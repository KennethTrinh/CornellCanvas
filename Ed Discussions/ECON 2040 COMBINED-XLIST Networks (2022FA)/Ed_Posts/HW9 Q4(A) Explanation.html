This is an example of a game in which there are only two pure Nash Equilibria and no mixed. It really depends on the payoffs/setup of the game, so it is worth it to check for both pure and mixed if asked. There are examples in which we analyze mixed strategies and ESS (see chapter 7.5 in the book. https://www.cs.cornell.edu/home/kleinber/networks-book/networks-book-ch07.pdf). We call (Y,Y) weakly dominated strategies (because of the equivalent payoff that you mention). However, this does not exclude it from being a pure strategy Nash Equilibrium. Check out chapter 6 pg 192 and the Stag Hunt game for an example of this ( https://www.cs.cornell.edu/home/kleinber/networks-book/networks-book-ch07.pdf).
