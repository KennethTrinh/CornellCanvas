Starting with assignment A1, CMS will run an "auto-helper" whenever you submit code sufficiently before the due date. When this happens, you will receive an e-mail a few minutes later telling you if there were any major issues. I want to avoid the term "autograder" - this service does not grade your assignment or thoroughly check its correctness. What it does do is detect major mistakes with the "mechanics" of submission: Is your character encoding correct? (be sure you set the encoding to "UTF-8" if you run Windows) Did you accidentally import any extra packages you're not allowed to use? Did you misspell a method name in both your class and your test? Did you upload a blank file? (all of these things happen multiple times each semester, and they slow down our ability to provide you with feedback). Historically, the auto-helper turns off the day the assignment is due - we want everyone to get an early start on assignments, and for any detailed debugging you might be doing the last day, the auto-helper won't help with anyway (you need to write your own tests for that). In summary, to access the auto-helper (starting with A1), just submit a draft of your assignment early on CMS, and you'll get an e-mail with the results.
