Did you mean to say b[0..-1] ? We need some notation to refer to the "empty range", and since our range notation's endpoints are inclusive, the only way to do that is to allow the bounds to be "inverted". However, we don't want to allow arbitrary inverted bounds; we just want to extend the pattern (as shown above).

Since many loops change a bound by +/- 1 each time, this notation well come in handy as we start to develop algorithms. We will find situations where we have a range b[i..j] where i==j , and the next step either increments i or decrements j , and we need the next step to be left with an empty range. This notation allows this naturally.
