Regarding your revised question, computers don't magically do what you expect; they only do what you tell them to do.

GameComponent knows nothing about the score label, which is constructed by GameMain and never shared with game . It could have been shared with game , but that would have been a different design (which is discussed briefly in the handout). Instead of having the game update the label directly, we use the "observer pattern" to have the label's owner listen for and react to changes to the score.

When designing larger computer programs, there are often many different ways to accomplish the same task, often with different tradeoffs. Our goal is to expose you to various design patterns throughout the assignments so you get a taste of what the options and their consequences are. Some designs seem much less straightforward (like not updating the score label in the same method that updates the score), but they often reduce coupling , which makes components more reusable (for example, you could add the gameboard to a different window that didn't use a JLabel for the score and instead presented it in a different way).
