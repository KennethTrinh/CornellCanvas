Author: Curran Muhlberger (admin)

I am sorry you feel this way. However, we have to juggle multiple constraints regarding when the AutoHelper is open, and in this case, leaving it open up until the deadline was not an option. At least for A4, the closing time for the AutoHelper was shown on CMS (this was never the case in previous semesters - we do try to improve). A reminder about the purpose of the AutoHelper: it is an integration smoke test . That is, we try plugging your code into our systems and see if anything catches fire. Its focus is on interfaces , not correctness: are your classes in the right package? did you spell your method names correctly? can your source code be read on non-Windows computers? Catching these mistakes early makes the grading process much smoother - graders can focus on correctness, rather than compatibility. But passing such a smoke test really shouldn't give you much confidence in the correctness of your code - you need to develop that confidence yourself via careful reading of the specifications and thorough testing. There are logistical realities regarding CMS, late submissions, and students' submission discipline that complicate when the AutoHelper can and can't be available (if the AutoHelper were to lead to more submission mistakes and confusion, then its benefit to smooth grading would be lost). We have some ideas to improve the situation in the future, but as with any software change that affects a large number of people, we need to act carefully. And if students are relying on the AutoHelper to give them confidence in their correctness or in the coverage of their testing, that's a worrying sign (it simply is not designed to do that). Regardless, we always recommend getting an early start on projects, and if the AutoHelper isn't encouraging that, we'll need to revisit it to remove that dissonance.
