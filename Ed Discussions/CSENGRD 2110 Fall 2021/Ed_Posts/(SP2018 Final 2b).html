Author: David Gries (admin)

The word "ascending" is often meant to mean that there can be duplicates. Thus, 2, 3, 3, 4, 5 could be considered an ascending sequence. But it IS ambiguous; for some, that sequence is not ascending. A better word to use in this case is non-descending, non-decreasing. The problem is that Merriam-Webster online does not define "non-descending. It has to be written as "non descending".
