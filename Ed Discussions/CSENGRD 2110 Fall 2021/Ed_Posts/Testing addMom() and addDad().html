Good thinking. Yes, you should test those things.

And there is nothing wrong with testing the pointers.

Suppose you did this:

Elephant dad= new Elephant("dad", 'M', 2040, 6); kid1.addDad(dad);

Then you should test whether kid1.dad() is equal to dad.

Just use an assertEquals statement for that. dad is the expected value; the other is the computed value.

You should also check that dad's number of children increased by 1!
