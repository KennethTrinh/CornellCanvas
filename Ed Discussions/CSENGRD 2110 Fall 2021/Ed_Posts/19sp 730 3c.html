A remark about precise language here: p is not cast to anything, and the word "created" could be interpreted as either construction or declaration. On the line in question, a variable p is declared , with static type Plane , and then the (address of the) value referenced in variable ob is assigned to p . A downcast is applied to the expression ob so that the static types of the right-hand-side and left-hand-side are compatible. So it is ob that is cast to Plane .

Do you remember the Compile-time Reference Rule? Memorize it! Please! It is is important to know the rules and principles and use them in answering questions. The expression ob.serialNum won't compile because of the Compile-time Reference Rule: field serialNum is not declared in class Object. that is why the case down to Plane is necessary.
