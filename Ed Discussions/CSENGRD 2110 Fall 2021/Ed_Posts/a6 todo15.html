The println() call, along with the try-with-resources structure (which automatically calls close() when its body finishes executing) is sufficient to write data to the file. You should be able to open the file in a text editor (like Eclipse) and see the string you wrote. I'm afraid I don't know about your grey file options - different operating systems can behave differently, or perhaps the files in your folder all have some special property. Consulting or office hours may provide better help in this area, since then they could see your screen and suggest different directories to browse to.
