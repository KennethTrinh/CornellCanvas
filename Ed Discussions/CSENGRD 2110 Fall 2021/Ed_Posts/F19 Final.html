Yes, f(x) = 2 and g(x) = 1 is a goo example.

But remember the definition of f(x) in O(g(x)): There exists c>0 and N>0 such that f(x) <= c*g(x) for x>= N.

So, take c + 3, for example, and

f(x) = 2 <= c*g(x) = 3*1 = 3 for all x.

O(2) and O(1) are the same sets
