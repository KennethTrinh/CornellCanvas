Dear Professors, TAs, and All, I hope you are safe during this particularly difficult time; the combination of finals, the Omicron variant, and volatile plans to get home, have amounted to uncertainty and immense stress. We have seen 214 new positive cases yesterday, totaling 469 cases, according to the school's COVID-19 Tracking dashboard. Despite the high vaccination rate, we are experiencing a 3% rate over the week, and 7% over the last two days. It is understood that Balch Hall, as well as hotels, are at our nearing their capacity. According to the dashboard, Red Zone (High Risk) is "significant increase in incidence with limited quarantine, isolation and/or local hospital capacity." I know many peers who are leaving early to protect themselves and their family members. At this point, many of us know of at least one friend who was contact traced. As advised by Housing Residential Life, newly positive students are now being instructed to isolate themselves in their rooms - an unideal situation for any suite/floor member. Clearly, the situation has dramatically worsened. Many students have the opportunity to take their exams online, or not at all. This allows them the flexibility to safely return home without having to remain on campus a moment longer. Respectfully, I am wondering what the plan for tomorrow's exam will be. There are over 350 students in this class, and I, as well as others I am sure, do not feel comfortable spending hours in the same hall, no matter the "precautions" put into place.
