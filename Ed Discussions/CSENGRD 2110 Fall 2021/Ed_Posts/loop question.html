Author: Varun Gande (student)

Vote Count: 1

No you don’t want to change t at all. What is now in b[t] was originally in b[j] (which we don’t know anything about). We shouldn’t worry about t in the else case, and it won’t get skipped over. You only worry about t when the element b[t] <= x, then you add 1 to t to shift the line over by one.
