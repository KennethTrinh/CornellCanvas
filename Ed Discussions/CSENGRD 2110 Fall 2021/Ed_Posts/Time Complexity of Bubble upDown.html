O(logn) is the height of the heap because the heap-tree is complete; it has all nodes on all levels except the bottom one and all nodes on the bottom level are al the way to the left.

O(logn) is the height of the heap.

Here, log(9) = 3.

3 is the height of this tree.

If we want to bubble up an element beginning at the bottom of the tree, it will take 3 switches to get to the top.
