Why does it look like b[h...j -1] when b[h + 1 ... t] describes values less than x?

----------- REPLIES -----------

Author: David Gries (admin)

Vote Count: 0

According to the invariant, b[h..t-1] contains values <= x, not b[h+1..t]. As long as b[t..j] contains a value, i.e. as long as t <= j, at least one more iteration is necessary. The loop ends when t = j+1. In that case, b[t..j] is empty, and b[j] is the element before the section of >=x values, which means that b[j] is the last element of the <=x values. Compare carefully invariant P and postcondition R. When do they look the same? When the ? section is empty.
