Do you know how to get "4" for question 1? If so, the same applies to question 2. One of the threads is not synchronized, so in the analogy, it ignores the "occupied" sign on the door and barges in through the broken latch. Synchronization is an opt-in, cooperative mechanism for preventing race conditions in critical sections of code. If a thread doesn't cooperate, then no protection is provided. This is why encapsulation is critical when writing data structures for safe concurrent use.
