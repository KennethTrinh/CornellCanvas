Author: Curran Muhlberger (admin)

The color bar is a convenient visual indicator, but the real information is in the text; e.g. "Runs: 4/4 Errors: 0 Failures: 1". You should confirm that the "4/4" matches the number of tests you think you have written (if it doesn't, you probably forgot an @Test annotation) and that the numbers of "Errors" and "Failures" are both 0. If you have an error or a failure, expand the list of tests in the sidebar and look at the tiny "x" and "checkmark" icons to see which one(s) failed and why.

Knowing exactly what tests are being run and which are passing gives you much more insight into your program than a bar of colors.
