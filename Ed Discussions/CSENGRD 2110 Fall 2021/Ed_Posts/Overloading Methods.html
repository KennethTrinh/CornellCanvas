Author: Curran Muhlberger (admin)

Vote Count: 1

For over loading , the names of the methods must match, but their parameters must be different (in order, number, type, etc.). Their return types may be different, but that alone is insufficient (since overloaded dispatch is determined based on argument static types). For over riding , the names of the methods must match, the parameter types must match, and the return type must be a subtype of the original. Use the @Override annotation to catch mistakes. (An override's parameter types must be equivalent to the originals, not just supertypes, because they affect static dispatch resolution for over loads ).
