"For a settled node s, at least one shortest v-s path contains only settled nodes and d[s] is its distance." I feel like this is saying that there's other shortest paths from v to s except those paths don't contain just settled nodes... Is it the shortest, though, if there's other short paths?

Author: David Gries (admin)

Vote Count: 1

There could be 4 different ways to get from v to s, all of length 100, and all other ways to get from v to s have length > 100. So there are 4 shortest paths. That's why we can't always talk about THE shortest path from v to s. The sentence is trying to convey that 1. for s in the settled set, d[s] IS the length of the shortest path from v to s over ALL paths. 2. At least one of those shortest paths contains only nodes in the settled set. I hope this helps.
