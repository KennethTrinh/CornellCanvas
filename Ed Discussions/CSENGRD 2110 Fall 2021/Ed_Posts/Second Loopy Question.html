You know that P is true:

s = sum of b[0..k-1] and m <= k <= n

You want to stop when R is true:

s = sum of b[0..n-1] and m <= k <= n

Compare what is true, P, with what you want, R. If k = n, they say the same thing.

You can see from P and R that when the loop terminates, k = n makes sense. For

k = n and P implies s is the sum of m..n-1, which is what we want.

Therefore, the loop should continue as long as k != n.

We know that k is increasing, therefore, instead of k != n we can use k < n.
