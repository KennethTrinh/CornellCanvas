Interfaces Iterable and Iterator are different - they declare different abstract methods. Look them up in the API documentation - one declares a method named iterator() , while the other declares methods like hasNext() and next() .

An Iterator is a mutable type for tracking your current location in a collection, but the for-each loop is for iterating over an entire collection.
