We defined a $k$ dimensional manifold in $\mathbb R^n$ to be a set $M \subseteq \mathbb R^n$ such that every point $\vec a\in M$, has an open neighborhood $V$ such that $M \cap V$ is the graph of a $C^1$ function from $\mathbb R^k$ to $\mathbb R^{n-k}$. How should we interpret this definition if $n = k$ as is the case for $B_R(\vec 0)$ in $\mathbb R^3$ in 5.2.3?

Author: Reyer Sjamaar (admin)

Vote Count: 1

$M\cap V$ is the graph of a $C^1$ function $f\colon U\to\mathbf{R}^{n-k}$, where $U$ is open in $\mathbf{R}^k$.

If $n=k$ this means $f\colon U\to\mathbf{R}^0=\{0\}$ is a constant function, whose graph is just the open set $U$ in $\mathbf{R}^n$. So a manifold of dimension $n$ in $\mathbf{R}^n$ just means: an open subset of $\mathbf{R}^n$.
