If we have a piece-with-boundary of a manifold M then the orientation of M defines the orientation of the smooth boundary by Definition 6.6.21. But if we start with the boundary orientation how can we get the orientation of the bounded region?

Author: Reyer Sjamaar (admin)

Vote Count: 0

Let $\mathbf{x}$ be a smooth boundary point of a piece-with-boundary $X$ of an orientable $k$-manifold $M$. Suppose you've oriented the boundary. Choose a positively oriented (i.e. direct) basis $\mathbf{v}_1,\dots,\mathbf{v}_{k-1}$ of the tangent space to the boundary $T_{\mathbf{x}}\partial_M^sX$. Choose an outward pointing vector $\mathbf{v}_0\in T_{\mathbf{x}}X$. Then $\mathbf{v}_0,\mathbf{v}_1,\dots,\mathbf{v}_{k-1}$ is a positively oriented basis of the tangent space to the boundary $T_{\mathbf{x}}X$. This defines the orientation of $M$.
