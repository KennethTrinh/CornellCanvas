If $f$ and $g$ are equal on all inputs (assuming their domains/codomains are equal), they are equal as functions, so their integrals are also clearly equal (one could just replace $f$ with $g$ everywhere). Recall that at a basic level functions are also sets, so the condition you've provided is equivalent to their equality as sets.

As for your second question, you should briefly justify this with the linearity of the integral.
