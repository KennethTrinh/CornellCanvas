No. Definition 5.3.1 only defines volume of a manifold. Our $X$ is not necessarily a manifold, but it is a subset of the manifold $S$. See the last line of Definition 5.3.2 for the definition of volume of a subset of a manifold.

So we have two manifolds, $S$ and $M$, and two subsets, $X\subseteq S$ and $P(X)\subseteq M$. You must show that $X$ and $P(X)$ have the same area ($2$-dimensional volume) in the sense of Definition 5.3.2.
