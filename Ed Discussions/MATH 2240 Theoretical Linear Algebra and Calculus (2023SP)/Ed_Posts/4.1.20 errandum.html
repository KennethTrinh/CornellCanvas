The problem says, "using upper and lower sums, compute the integral...". That is what you have to do, not something else. There may be different ways of computing the integral, but that is not what this problem is about.

In an upper sum $U_N(f)$ or a lower sum $L_N(f)$ all but finitely many dyadic cubes $C_{\mathbf{k},N}$ contribute $0$ to the sum. Deciding which cubes contribute a nonzero term to the sum requires some delicate book-keeping. That is what this problem is about.
