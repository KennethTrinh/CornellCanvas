Definition 6.2.1 is flawed. $V$ is the domain of the $k$-form $\phi$ and $U$ is the domain of the map $\gamma\colon U\to V$. As you rightly point out, $U$ is not open in many of the examples and exercises.

So we must extend Def 6.2.1 to the case where $U$ is not necessarily open. Two difficulties arise:

1. What do we mean by a $C^1$-map $\gamma\colon U\to V$ if $U$ is not open?

2. What do we mean by

$(**)\quad\int_\gamma\phi=\int_U\phi_{\gamma(\mathbf{u})}\bigl(D_1\gamma(\mathbf{u}),\dots,D_k\gamma(\mathbf{u})\bigr)\,|d\mathbf{u}|$?

Answer:

1. A map $\gamma\colon U\to V$ whose domain is an arbitrary subset $U\subseteq\mathbf{R}^k$ is called $C^1$ if there exist an open subset $O\subseteq\mathbf{R}^k$ and a $C^1$-map $\tilde{\gamma}\colon O\to V$ such that $U\subseteq O$ and $\gamma=\tilde\gamma$ on $U$.

2. The identity (**) is the defininition of $\int_\gamma\phi$, where the right-hand side is to be interpreted as a Riemann integral. In particular we require the function $\mathbf{u}\mapsto\boldsymbol{1}_U(\mathbf{u})\phi_{\gamma(\mathbf{u})}\bigl(D_1\gamma(\mathbf{u}),\dots,D_k\gamma(\mathbf{u})\bigr)$ to be Riemann integrable over $\mathbf{R}^k$.

$k$-form fields are defined over open sets as in Definition 6.1.16, so $V$ is the open set that the $k$-form field $\varphi$ is defined over. Recall the common theme that smooth functions are often defined with open domains.

As for the $U$s defined in Exercise 6.2.3, for now I would suggest that you integrate over the interior of $U$ instead of $U$ itself, as this shouldn't change the $k$-dimensional measure of the region you're integrating over, but I would wait to see what Prof. Sjamaar says.
