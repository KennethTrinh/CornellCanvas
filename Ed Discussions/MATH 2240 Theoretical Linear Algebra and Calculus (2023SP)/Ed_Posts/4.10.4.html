Must the Lipschitz condition of the derivative of the change of variables map be satisfied across all of $\mathbb R^3$ or just within the bounded region we find the volume of?

Author: Reyer Sjamaar (admin)

Vote Count: 0

The change of variables theorem as stated in the book requires the Lipschitz condition on $D\Phi$ on some open neighborhood $U$ of the set $X$. (The region we are calculating the volume of is $\Phi(X)$.) Recall that Lipschitz conditions on $D\Phi$ often come for free, namely for maps $\Phi$ that are $C^2$ (Proposition 2.8.9).
