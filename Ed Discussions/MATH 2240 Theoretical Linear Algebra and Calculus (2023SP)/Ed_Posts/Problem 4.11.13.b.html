Author: Bryan Lu (admin)

Vote Count: 0

No, I'm not sure that you can say that. I'm thinking of the polynomials as formal sums in $x$, not as functions. In particular, view the polynomial $a_0 + a_1 x + a_2 x^2 + \dots + a_n x^n$ as the vector $\begin{bmatrix} a_0 \\ a_1 \\ \vdots \\ a_n \end{bmatrix}$ in $\mathbb{R}^{n+1}$. Then use the metric on $\mathbb{R}^{n+1}$ to define the metric on the polynomials of degree $\leq n$.
