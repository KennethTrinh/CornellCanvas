Author: Reyer Sjamaar (admin)

Vote Count: 0

For $0$-forms (i.e. functions) $f$ we checked that $df=Df$. In other words, $df$ is the row vector $\begin{pmatrix}\partial f/\partial y_1&\cdots&\partial f/\partial y_n\end{pmatrix}$.

Taking $f(\mathbf{y})=y_i$ gives $dy_i=\mathbf{e}_i^T$, which is how we previously defined the elementary $1$-form $dy_i$.

So the two definitions are consistent.
