Let $\mathcal{P}_N$ be the paving of the unit interval $[0,1]$ into subintervals $[k/N,(k+1)/N]$. You observe correctly that the sequence of pavings $\mathcal{P}_N$ is not nested. But it has a property that is almost as good, namely that for each $N_1$ and $N_2$ there exists $N$ such that $\mathcal{P}_N$ is a common refinement of $\mathcal{P}_{N_1}$ and $\mathcal{P}_{N_2}$. (Take $N$ to be the least common multiple of $N_1$ and $N_2$.) We say that $\mathcal{P}_N$ is a directed system of pavings. You can use this to show that the above limit exists and is equal to the Riemann integral if $f$ is Riemann integrable.
