You are misreading the question. First of all, $A$ is not an $n\times n$-matrix, but an $n\times k$-matrix. Secondly, the cubes $C_k$ and $C_k'$ are not in $\mathbf{R}^n$, but in $\mathbf{R}^k$.

The unit cube $C_k$ is the parallelotope spanned by the $k$ standard basis vectors in $\mathbf{R}^k$ ( not in $\mathbf{R}^n$). The other cube $C_k'$ is the parallelotope spanned by some other $k$-tuple of edge vectors $\mathbf{c}_1,\dots,\mathbf{c}_k$ in $\mathbf{R}^k$.
