Author: Reyer Sjamaar (admin)

Vote Count: 0

No. $X$ need not be a manifold and we haven't even defined what it means for a non-manifold to be parametrized.

Use Definition 5.3.2 (where the subset is called $Y$): the $k$-dimensional volume of a subset $X$ of a $k$-dimensional manifold $M$ is well-defined if the indicator function $\boldsymbol{1}_X$ is R-integrable over $M$.
