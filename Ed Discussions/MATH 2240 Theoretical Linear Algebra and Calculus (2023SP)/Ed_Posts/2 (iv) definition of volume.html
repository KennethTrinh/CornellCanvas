Since $\operatorname{vol}_k$ is defined on $(\mathbb R^n)^k$ and $P(\vec a_1 ,\dots, \vec a_k)$ is a subset of $\mathbb R^n$, which is not an element of $(\mathbb R^n)^k$, how should we interpret $\operatorname{vol}_k(P(\vec a_1 ,\dots, \vec a_k))$?

----------- REPLIES -----------

Author: Reyer Sjamaar (admin)

Vote Count: 1

Define the volume of the parallelotope to be $\operatorname{vol}_k$ of its edge vectors, i.e. $\operatorname{vol}_k(P(\vec a_1 ,\dots, \vec a_k))=\operatorname{vol}_k(\vec a_1 ,\dots, \vec a_k)$
