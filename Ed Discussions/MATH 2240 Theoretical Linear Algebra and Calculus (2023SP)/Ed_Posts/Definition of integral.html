Did we define $\int_a^bf(x)dx=-\int_b^af(x)dx$? Because otherwise the definition with the indicator function gets weird. Or do we assume that the upper bound is bigger than the lower bound and discard the rest of the cases? Otherwise, $F(x)$ from 4.11.8 wouldn't make any sense for $x<0$.

Yes, if $b\le a$ we put $\int_a^bf(x)\,dx=-\int_b^af(x)\,dx$.  This is an example of an “oriented” integral, of which we will see more in the next chapter.
