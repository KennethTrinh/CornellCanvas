Let me paraphrase the question. Suppose we have a $C^1$-map $\gamma\colon(a,b)\to\mathbf{R}^n$. When is the image $M=\gamma((a,b))$ a manifold in $\mathbf{R}^n$?

The answer is "yes" if all the following conditions are satisfied:

  • $\gamma$ is injective;

  • $\gamma'(t)\ne\mathbf{0}$ for all $t$;

  • the map $\gamma^{-1}\colon M\to(a,b)$ is continuous.

    • Conditions 1 and 2 say that $\gamma$ is a strict parametrization (i.e. a parametrization in the sense of chapter 3), but that turns out to be not quite enough for $M$ to be a manifold. You need the extra condition 3.
