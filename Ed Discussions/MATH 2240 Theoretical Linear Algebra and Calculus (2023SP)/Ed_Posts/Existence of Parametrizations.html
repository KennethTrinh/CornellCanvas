Suppose $M$ is a $k$ dimensional manifold in $\mathbb R^n$, $M'\subseteq M$ has $k$ dimensional volume $0$, and $M\backslash M'$ is still a $k$ dimensional manifold. If $f:M\to \mathbb R$ and $$ I =\int_{M\backslash M'} f(\mathbf{x})\left|d^k \mathbf{x}\right| $$ exists, is there any way to use Theorem 5.2.6 (every manifold has a parametrization) to conclude that $$ \int_{M} f(\mathbf{x})\left|d^k \mathbf{x}\right| $$ also exists and is equal to $I$ without finding an actual parametrization of $M$?

It depends on what you mean by "the integrals exist". If you interpret the integrals as Lebesgue integrals, the answer to your question is "yes", but that isn't so easy to prove. If you want Riemann integrals, I'm not sure if the answer is still "yes".
