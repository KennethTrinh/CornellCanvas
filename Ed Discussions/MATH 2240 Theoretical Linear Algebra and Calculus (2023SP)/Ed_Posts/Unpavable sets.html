To begin with, a pavable set must be bounded. An unbounded set cannot be pavable. Among bounded sets, say subsets of the unit interval $[0,1]$, the following are not pavable: $\mathbf{Q}\cap[0,1]$ (the set of rationals between $0$ and $1$), $(\mathbf{R}\backslash\mathbf{Q})\cap[0,1]$ (the set of irrationals between $0$ and $1$), and the Cantor set. More generally, let $A$ be a bounded subset of $\mathbf{R}^n$ such that $A$ is dense in $\bar{A}$ and $\bar{A}\backslash A$ is dense in $\bar{A}$. Then $A$ is not pavable.
