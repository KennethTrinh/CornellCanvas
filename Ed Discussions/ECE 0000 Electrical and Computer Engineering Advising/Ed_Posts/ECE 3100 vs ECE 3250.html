They're not that similar content wise. ECE 3100 is a probability theory course. This is useful if you want to take courses in machine learning and information theory (so ECE 4110, ECE 5630, CS 4780, etc) ECE 3250 is a signal processing course. While there are applications to machine learning and vision within signal processing, it's not exactly the same as a course in probability (at least to my understanding)

Having taught both courses, I can state with confidence that they share little in common. Your best bet: check pre-requisites for downstream courses that interest you, and plan accordingly. FWIW if you find the signals/systems/math/ML/learning side of ECE appealing, both courses would benefit you. Remember that extra ECE Foundation Courses count as 3000+ ECE Electives.
