I personally don't know why one group's window has to close when the next group's opens. I can imagine a system in which seniors can still adjust their schedules while juniors, sophomores, and first-years are enrolling, and so on down the line. Why they don't do it that way I don't know. This is a question for the registrar's office. The good news is that most of our classes can accommodate arbitrary numbers of students. We can keep raising the cap as necessary. So if you want to take 2720, 3100, or 3150 next term, it's a pretty safe bet that you will get in. There are some exceptions, the most notable being ECE 1210. The flipped nature of that class means that it has to be taught in certain rooms that have certain capacities. Once that class is full it is full. There are a few other classes like that, but most have elastic capacities.
