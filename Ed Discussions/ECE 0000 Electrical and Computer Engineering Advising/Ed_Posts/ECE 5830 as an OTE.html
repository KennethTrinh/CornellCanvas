I suggest petitioning it. I don't remember the details of the discussion, but I do remember personally not being on board with some of the rationale. Similar "reasoning" arose when ECE 2400 appeared and hadn't yet been approved as an ENGRD. The committee decided that students could count it as OTE but that in doing so they would "eat up" their one allowed ECE OTE. I didn't care for that outcome, either.

My attitude: ECE 5830 is like a business course. ECE 2400 is like a coding course. Those courses' orbits arguably share more with, say, Dyson and with Bowers CIS than they do with ECE.
