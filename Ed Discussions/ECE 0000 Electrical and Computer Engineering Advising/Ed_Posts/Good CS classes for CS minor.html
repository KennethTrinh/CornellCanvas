Hi! CS major (+ECE minor) here!

I would highly recommend CS 3110. I think that 3110 makes you a better programmer. Functional programming is a different way of looking at code and concepts, and you can learn a lot from it.

Additionally, OS (CS 4410) is great and counts as an ECE technical elective for ECE requirements (and I've heard the practicum, 4411 is fun), though it can be hard to get into if you're not a CS major, but I've heard that the waitlist clears most years.

I also really enjoyed compilers (CS 4120/4121, you must take the course and the practicum at the same time), and taking it along with Computer Architecture (4750) really shows how almost all aspects of computers work. Fair warning though, it's a probably the most difficult course I've taken at Cornell and I'd definitely take 3110 before compilers.
