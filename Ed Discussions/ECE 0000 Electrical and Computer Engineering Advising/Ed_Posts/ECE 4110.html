Author: Aaron Wagner (admin)

The 3100 prereq for 4110 is pretty strict since 4110 directly extends what you learn in 3100. So you can enroll in 4110, but be prepared to learn two semester's worth of material in one semester. Also be advised that probability as a subject does not lend itself well to cramming --- it requires a whole different way of thinking compared to other math and physics courses. My experience is that it takes about a semester for the material to really gel for most students. And ECE 4110 will assume it has already gelled for you from day 1. I taught ECE 4110 many years ago, and I did have one student who enrolled without 3100 (or ENGRD 2700 or similar) who did very well. So it is possible. But he was an atypical student. 4110 usually begins by summarizing all of 3100 in about two weeks . You could attend the 4110 lectures for the first week or so and judge how feasible it is for you.
