Okay, regarding the ECE Foundation Courses:

= Two represent the more applied-math side of ECE: ECE 3250 and ECE 3100

= Two represent the more applied-physics side of ECE: ECE 3030 and ECE 3150

= One represents the computer-systems side of ECE: ECE 3140

I suggest looking at higher-level stuff and choose Foundation Courses by considering pre-requisites and where your interests lie. You need to take one of the physics-centric ones and one of the math-centric ones eventually. Many students take four, some take all five --- extras work as 3000+ ECE Electives.

3030 and 3250 happen only in the fall; the others happen only in the spring. if you wanted to take two this coming fall, it would be 3030 and 3250.
