ECE 4240 is a cross-listed course from Mechanical Engineering. You can tell because the catalog description says "For description, see MAE 4810." This means that it is basically an MAE class (MAE 4810) that is deemed to have enough ECE content that whenever MAE decides to offer it, it appears automatically in our roster as an ECE class. This is so that our students can count it toward ECE graduation requirements without having to petition.

The upshot is that we have no say or insight into when this class is offered. It is entirely up to MAE.

This Ed Discussion was originally inspired by a similar site set up by Prof. Kirby for MAE. On the MAE site, Prof. Kirby posted the following recently [text is square brackets is my addition]

"hello all --
MAE4810, which was originally planned to be taught in F22, is being moved to S23. Apologies to all affected. @Kae-Lynn [Kae-Lynn Buchanan Wilson, one of MAE's undergraduate coordinators] will be sending out emails to enrolled students to make sure they are aware.
I don't want to preempt announcements that are the purview of others to announce, but just so you know why this is happening: prof ferrari [the instructor for MAE 4810] will be taking on an exciting new role in the college, which I think will be announced by the dean shortly (next week?). This new role requires that we move the term in which 4810 is taught."

I just consulted the 2020, 2021, 2022 editions of the handbooks, and none of them list MAE 4810 (or ECE 4240) as part of the robotics minor. When you say the new handbook doesn't list ECE 4240, are you indicating that the older editions did? If so, could you point me to where?

Actually, the Courses of Study (courses.cornell.edu) is generally considered the most authoritative statement of requirements, then the handbook, with college and dept. websites being last. The websites are not vetted by as many people, and they can be changed at any point, with no (guaranteed) option to access the old version. In contrast, the Courses of Study is checked by more people, and it is published once per year, with all previous versions being available so you can always see the requirements that were in effect when you entered, which is what matters to you.

In this case, MAE 4810 is actually listed in the courses of study, so it counts toward the minor. The problem is that it isn't listed in the handbook. I'll point this out to people who manage the minor. Thanks for pointing this out.
