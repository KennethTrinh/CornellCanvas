I'd like to call your attention to a late addition to the Fall 2022 course roster: ECE 4960, Special Topics in Electrical and Computer Engineering: CMOS Integrated Sensors, Actuators and Circuits. Here is a short description: "Sensors and Actuators are integrated directly on CMOS wafers and are controlled by CMOS circuits. This class will describe MEMS-CMOS integration approaches for electrostatic and piezoelectric devices. Circuit design for managing sensors with respect to increasing stability, linearity, and SNR will be covered. Interface circuits such as ADCs and DACs will be covered in the context of noise added to the sensor signals." I'm expecting that a graduate version will be added to the roster as well. If you are interested in the graduate version, you can enroll in ECE 4960 now to reserve a spot and then move to the graduate version when it is created. For more information about the content of the course, contact the instructor, Prof. Lal. For logistical questions about the expected graduate version, please contact me.
