Attached is a PDF of the FA23 ECE class roster. Thanks to Ronin Sharma and especially Aidan McNay, the roster now has a calendar view on the first page. It also combines the listing of co-meeting classes and has a few other improvements.

There are several special topics offerings that are new for next semester:

  • ECE 4950: Sensor Design with Prof. El-Ghazaly

  • ECE 4950: Quantum Information Science: Communication and Computation with Prof. Wilde

  • ECE 6950: Atomic and Quantum Optical Physics and Technology with Prof. Mehta

    • In addition, we are offering ECE 4330: Microwave Theory, Devices, and Applications (which will be renamed to Microwave Circuit Design when the catalog is updated) with Prof. Ibrahim. ECE 4330 is not technically a new class, but it has not been offered in a while, and Prof. Ibrahim is revamping it. So it feels new.

    There is a mandate from the College and the Registrar that all departments spread their courses out over the day and week. With larger incoming student classes, there simply aren't enough classrooms to go around otherwise. So you'll notice more 8 a.m., 9 a.m., 8:40 a.m., and evening classes across all departments, including ECE. One side benefit of this is fewer class conflicts.

    Re: conflicts, bear in mind that moving classes around inevitably creates new conflicts even as it eliminates others. If you see a conflict that will prevent students from graduating on time or that you expect will impact a large number of students, please do bring it to my attention. We are not technically allowed to change the roster at this point, but I'm sure we can do so if there is a serious problem. But the bar is pretty high.

    I had hopes to post this earlier, along with a survey of which classes people wish to enroll in. We would then schedule classes to eliminate the conflicts as identified in the survey. That didn't happen this round for a variety of reasons. Hopefully in the fall.

    For sensor design, no. For quantum information science, uncertain but more likely than not.

    Yes, syllabi in April.

    The classes.cornell.edu site for FA23 is indeed not public yet. ECE has no control over that.

    Re: special topics numbering, ECE used to place different special topics courses offered during a given semester under different numbers (ECE 4950, 4960, etc.) to avoid confusion. The registrar told us it is a best practice to list them all as sections of one course number. In my experience, this tends to create confusion both for them and for us, but there are probably reasons to do it this way. I expect that over time the other numbers like 4960, etc., will disappear from the catalog completely.

    Re: 5250 and 5010, correct. But it is hard to find a time for 5010 that doesn't conflict with any 5000-level course and meets various other constraints. For the case of 5250 and 5010, you might be able to work something out with the instructors. 5010 is only 7 weeks, and it only conflicts with the 5250 discussion.
