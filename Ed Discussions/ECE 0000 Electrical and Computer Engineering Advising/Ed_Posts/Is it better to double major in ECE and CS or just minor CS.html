As a double-major, I think I probably should've just stuck with a minor in ECE, but I appreciate the breadth of courses I've had to take to fulfill both majors. I probably wouldn't have taken certain ECE courses that I've found have a surprising amount of application to my interests in CS.

If you're thinking of "worth it" in terms of if it will make you more employable or more appealing for graduate school, probably not. But if you want to just learn both subjects very deeply I think it is worth it for me at least.

Also disclaimer, ymmv because a double-major can look very different based on your electives. Most people focus a lot on computer engineering and take courses like CS 4410, ECE 4750, CS 4120, etc. Some people choose to take much more theoretical courses in both CS and ECE, and some people yet still choose to take more robotics oriented courses. All of these are ways of getting a "double major", but the skills that you develop will be quite different.
