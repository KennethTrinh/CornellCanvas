Hi everyone, I thought we'd try an experiment for this year's pre-enroll. I've posted below the ECE roster that we use internally, which is generated using an ECE-produced script from the University's master database. It organizes useful information about the ECE courses in one table. It also computes scheduling conflicts and notes them in the last column. I hope you find this roster useful for your course planning. I personally went through it looking for problematic conflicts, as did some of the instructors. But it is possible we missed something. Let me know if you spot a conflict that you think merits moving a course, bearing in mind that we cannot avoid all conflicts. It is officially past the deadline for us to move courses, but I'm sure we can do so if a serious conflict is spotted. A few other miscellaneous notes about Fall 2022: With the elimination of ECE 2200, ECE 3250 will become a stand-alone signals and systems course starting this Fall. So the Fall 2022 version will not have ECE 2200 as a prereq. In fact, starting Fall 2022, ECE 2200 will not be listed as a prereq for anything. As noted in recent postings, ECE 4250 is being offered in the Fall but it is losing its CDE status. I'm hoping to get some additional signals-type courses added to the list of CDEs, but I can't promise anything yet. The plan is to offer ECE 4670 as a CDE in Spring 2023 as usual. ECE 4200 and ECE 4740 are both courses that have been offered during the Fall during some past years that we are planning to offer Spring 2023 instead of Fall 2022. [updated 4/15/22]

------------------------------------

Author: Aaron Wagner (admin)

Vote Count: 0

No. They are two versions (undergrad and grad) of the same course. They have the same meeting patterns and are taught by the same instructor, but the expectations for students in the grad version are higher. Students in the grad version are usually responsible for more material, have extra assignments or more advanced problems to complete, etc. You can see that the classes are connected from their catalog entry. There are actually several examples of this (e.g., 4110/5110) in the roster. Undergrads should enroll in the 4000-level version in most cases.
