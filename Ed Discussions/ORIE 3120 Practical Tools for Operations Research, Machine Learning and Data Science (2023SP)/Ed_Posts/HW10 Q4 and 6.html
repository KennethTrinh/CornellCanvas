If you are letting q = the number of eggs to cook, you should indeed multiply by 3.

For 4, we want to interpret the cost of underage to be "how much extra do I have to pay per unit of inventory I have missing (unfulfilled demand)". In this case, it should be 15.

A good way to think about this is to figure out separate expressions for the cost in two cases: when Q > D and when Q < D, and relate that to the overage and underage expressions.
