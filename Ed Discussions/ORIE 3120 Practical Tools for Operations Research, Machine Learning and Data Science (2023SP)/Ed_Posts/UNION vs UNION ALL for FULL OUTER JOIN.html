Good question. I think the case that you are referring to is that B table has two exactly the same records. In that case, when you do the union after you right-join or left-join, you will have some duplicate records. But in that case, the Full Outer Join will also return two duplicate records, since each record in B (even though they are duplicate) is treated differently. In other words, you need to collect everything from either Table A and Table B for a Full Outer Join and Union All is still needed.
