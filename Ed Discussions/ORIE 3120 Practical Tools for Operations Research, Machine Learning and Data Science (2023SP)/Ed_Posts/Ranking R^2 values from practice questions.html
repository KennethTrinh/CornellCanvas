Influentiality is based on the value of the actual coefficients used to generate the data. You can imagine a feature with a coefficient of 100 is likely to explain more of the variation in the outcome variable as opposed to a covariate with a coefficient of 0.01, for example. Additionally, including multiple covariates as opposed to just one will always explain more of the variation, which is indicated by the increase in R^2.
