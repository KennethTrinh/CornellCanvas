We only have one observed instance of Y. Following the hint, we will consider a symmetric interval (with Y in the middle). Y has been taken as a datapoint sampled from the random distribution that is uniform about [θ-1,θ+1]. The density (pdf) of a random uniform variable is 1/(b-a) in the interval [a,b] and 0 outside. You are trying to compute a 95% confidence interval for θ, and you know that Y was taken from the uniform distribution about theta [θ-1, θ+1]. The interval will be in terms of Y, since that is all you know
