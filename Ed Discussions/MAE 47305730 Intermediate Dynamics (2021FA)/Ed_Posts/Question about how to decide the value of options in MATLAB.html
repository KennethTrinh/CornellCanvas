It is entirely context dependent. As you can see, finding something that produces mathematically pure periodic motion will require a high degree of accuracy. If you were trying to predict the flight of a ball, then your engineering judgement can be used to see how accurate the landing spot needs to be.

If you want to be more rigorous, you can also do a fidelity check, where you trial different levels of accuracy and record the progression towards the solution. Such a check is often done in CFD simulations, where the granularity of the mesh needs to be resolved such that the solution is sufficiently accurate without requiring excessive computation time.
