Strictly speaking, a dyad is the result of a multiplication of two vectors, specifically the result of an 'outer' product or 'tensor' product.

The 'outer' or 'tensor' product (two names for the same thing) are different from either the dot product or cross product. The dot product is also known as an 'inner' product, which is linguistically opposite of the outer product.

So now you can do:

  • Inner product: multiply the components that are in the same position and add up the result.

  • Cross product: multiply the components that are in strictly different positions, and add up the resulting components in those positions.

  • Outer Product: multiply everything by everything!
