So for a general system the Jacobian is defined as:

J_{ij} = \frac{\partial f_i}{\partial x_j}

where f_i are the functions and x_j are the variables. In the 2D dynamics case, f_i is going to be the 4 time derivatives -- so x-dot, y-dot, vx-dot, and vy-dot, and x_j is the 4 variables, so x, y, vx, and vy. This will be a 4-by-4 matrix:

J = \begin{bmatrix} \frac{\partial \dot{x}}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial \dot{x}}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial \dot{x}}{\partial v_x} & \frac{\partial \dot{x}}{\partial v_y}\\ \frac{\partial \dot{y}}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial \dot{y}}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial \dot{y}}{\partial v_x} & \frac{\partial \dot{y}}{\partial v_y}\\\frac{\partial \dot{v_x}}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial \dot{v_x}}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial \dot{v_x}}{\partial v_x} & \frac{\partial \dot{v_x}}{\partial v_y}\\\frac{\partial \dot{v_y}}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial \dot{v_y}}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial \dot{v_y}}{\partial v_x} & \frac{\partial \dot{v_y}}{\partial v_y} \end{bmatrix}

After finding the derivatives, evaluate the 16 functions (the 8 in the first 2 rows are simple) at the stable state [x,y,0,0] to get the final numerical value for the derivative. This should yield the 4 by 4 matrix with no variables that MatLab calculates with fsolve. When you have that, all that's left is to do a "simple" (or at least a single..) calculation to find the eigenvalues with det[J-λI] = 0, where λ are the eigenvalues to check for positivity to see if they're stable.

In the general case this calculation's a mess, so I'd recommend choosing the system carefully or using some analytical analysis tool.

Hope this helped and that it's not too late for the homework...
