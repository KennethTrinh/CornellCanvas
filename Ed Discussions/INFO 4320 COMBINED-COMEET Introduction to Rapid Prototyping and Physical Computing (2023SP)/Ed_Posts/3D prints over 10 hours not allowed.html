Hi, As mentioned in class, 3D prints over long hours have a high chance of failure, which would be a great waste of limited 3D printing resources. We do not encourage you to design and print large structures. For large flat/regular shapes , we recommend using the laser cutter , which is not only much faster but also more precise. For large complicated structures , we recommend breaking it down to different modules and printing them separately, which will give you more flexibility to iterate individual modules instead wasting time reprinting everything. 3D prints over 10 hours are not allowed . This rule will be randomly enforced: we will not specifically check the prints. However, if a staff member happens to see a print over 10 hours, he/she will stop the print. If you believe that you do need a large print, talk to your TA and we will make sure it won't be stopped by the staff (if it does not fail itself). In addition, we have put several SD cards and readers in Rhodes 114 for your convenience. They should not be taken out of 114. Please be considerate while using them and always put them back after use. Please let us know if you have any questions. Have a great weekend.
