How can we find T(1), T(t), T(t^2), and T(t^3) if we don't know what the outputs of p(-3), p(-1), p(1), and p(3) are?

----------- REPLIES -----------

Author: Phillip Jang (staff)

Vote Count: 0

You do know the outputs, as $1, t, t^2$ and $t^3$ are your $p(t)$'s here, and for each $p(t)$ you just plug in the four values of $t$ into each polynomial to get a column vector in $\mathbb{R}^4$. For example,

$$T(t^2) = \begin{bmatrix}(-3)^2 \\ (-1)^2 \\ (1)^2 \\ (3)^2\end{bmatrix}$$
