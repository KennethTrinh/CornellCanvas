A subspace is defined to have all the properties of a vector space as well as being a subset of one. Since it's already a subset of a vector space the only properties you have to reprove are that the subspace is closed under addition and scalar multiplication (that is, if you take any two matrices in the subspace and add them together, the result is still in the subspace and if you multiply any matrix in the subspace by a scalar then the result is still in the subspace). What you said is equivalent, although it is more precise/thorough to say it the way they did (since the subspace in question is diagonal matrices).
