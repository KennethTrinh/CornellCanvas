From my notes for Lecture 6 (Feb 19):

Theorem: Suppose that p is a solution of a nonhomogeneous system A x = b . Then the solution set consists of vectors of the form p + q for solutions q of the homogeneous system A x = b .

So essentially,

is the particular solution p of the equation A x = b and from part (c), we obtained the general solution to the homogeneous equation A x = 0 , we simply add the two to get the general solution for A x = b . The s and the t are arbitrary variable names for the weights; they could be anything.
