Since $\mathcal{B}=\{P,Q,R,S\}$ is a basis of $\mathbb{R}^{2 \times 2}$, every $2 \times 2$ matrix can be uniquely written as a linear combination of $P,Q,R,S$. You need to solve for the unique scalars $c_1,\ldots,c_4$ such that

$$E=\begin{bmatrix}1 & 4 \\ 8 & 3\end{bmatrix} = c_1P+c_2Q+c_3R+c_4S$$

These four coefficients are the $\mathcal{B}$-coordinates of $E$.

$$[E]_\mathcal{B} = \begin{bmatrix}c_1 \\ c_2 \\ c_3 \\ c_4\end{bmatrix}$$
