Yes. If $A$ has real entries and you know one eigenvalue $\lambda_1 = a+ib$ with eigenvector $v_1 = x+iy$, then the other eigenpair is automatically conjugate to the original. $\lambda_2 = \bar{\lambda_1} = a-ib$ and $v_2 = \bar{v}_1 = x-iy$. Hence if you've solved for $\lambda_1$ and $v_1$, you automatically get $\lambda_2$ and $v_2$ for free since $A$ has real entries. However, do make sure you use the eigenpair for $\lambda=a+ib$ and not $\lambda=a-ib$ in the general real solution or your minus signs will be in the wrong place. If you ended up using the other eigenpair, then you can rewrite it as $\lambda=a-ib=a+i(-b)$ and flip minus signs accordingly.
