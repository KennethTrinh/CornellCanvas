Is it possible to have a complex eigenvalue with a corresponding real eigenvector? I feel like the answer is no but I can't seem to come up with a good way to show why. Any explanation would be greatly appreciated :)

No. Suppose A x =w x where A and x are real, and x is nonzero. Some component of x is nonzero, say x_j. But that same component of A x is real and equals wx_j. Now divide by x_j to see that w must be real.
