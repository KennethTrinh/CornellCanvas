Why do we use the echelon form of A for forming a basis for A and not A itself?

----------- REPLIES -----------

Author: Ilya Amburg (staff)

Vote Count: 0

The first three rows of A could be linearly dependent so by picking the nonzero rows of the echelon form you are guaranteed to find the largest linearly independent set of rows that span the row space of the matrix (basis for the row space of A).
