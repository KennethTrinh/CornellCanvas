Notice that the formula for projection writes y-hat as a linear combination of the orthogonal basis vectors. We know if y is in the span of those vectors, then y can be expressed as a linear combination of them. Also, the projection of y onto the span is just y itself. So we can "project" y onto the span, yielding a linear combo of orthog basis vectors. So we use the same formula :)
