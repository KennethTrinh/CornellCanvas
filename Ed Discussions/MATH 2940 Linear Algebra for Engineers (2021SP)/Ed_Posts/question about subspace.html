The fact that a subspace must contain the zero vector essentially says two things: it is closed under scalar multiplication (another property of subspaces), and it is nonempty. Some examples: The xy plane is a subspace, and the zero vector within it is [0 0]. The zero vector in the subspace along the line y=2x is also [0 0]. The x-axis is a subspace, and the zero vector within it is [0].
