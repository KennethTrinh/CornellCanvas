I was wondering why we have to use the eigenvalue and corresponding eigenvector specifically with the form a-bi. Why can't we use the one in the form a+bi?

----------- REPLIES -----------

Author: Phillip Jang (staff)

Vote Count: 1

It's done this way so that the minus sign in the $C$ matrix of $A=PCP^{-1}$ appears in the right place. Note that $b$ can be a negative number too so if you choose the eigenvalue that looks like $3+2i = 3-(-2)i$ then you need to set $a=3$ and $b=-2$ and $C = \begin{bmatrix}a & -b \\ b & a\end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}3 & 2 \\ -2 & 3\end{bmatrix}$.
