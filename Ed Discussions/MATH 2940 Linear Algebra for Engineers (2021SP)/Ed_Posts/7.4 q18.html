Given that $A^{-1}=V \Sigma^{-1} U^T$, and $A$ is an $nxn$ square matrix and invertible, do we know that $\Sigma^{-1} = \frac{1}{\sigma_1 \sigma_2 ... \sigma_n} \Sigma$ since $\Sigma$ is $nxn$ and $A^T A$ cannot have eigenvalue $0$ because $A$ is invertible?

----------- REPLIES -----------

Author: Adrian Lewis (admin)

Vote Count: 0

No. The inverse of the diagonal matrix Sigma is a diagonal matrix with diagonal entries the reciprocals of the corresponding entries in Sigma.
