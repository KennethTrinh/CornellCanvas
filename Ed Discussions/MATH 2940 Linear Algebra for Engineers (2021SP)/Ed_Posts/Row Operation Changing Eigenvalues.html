Author: Phillip Jang (staff)

Subtracting $\lambda I$ from the row-replaced matrix $EA$ is not the same as row-replacing $A-\lambda I$. While equality of roots can be possible by chance, $det(EA-\lambda I) \neq det(E[A-\lambda I])$ in general. For example, these two row-replaced matrices (R1 <- R1-R2) have the same eigenvalues (1 with algebraic multiplicity 2) $$\begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 0 & 1\end{bmatrix} \& \begin{bmatrix}1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1\end{bmatrix}$$ but these matrices (R2 <- R2-2R1) have different eigenvalues ($1 \pm \sqrt{2}$ and $\{1,-1\}$ respectively) $$\begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 2 & 1\end{bmatrix} \& \begin{bmatrix}1 & 1 \\ 0 & -1\end{bmatrix}$$ Hence you cannot generally assume row-replacement preserves eigenvalues even if it sometimes happens.

If you're doing a row operation, you're left multiplying $A$ against some elementary matrix $E$ before subtracting $\lambda I$ so the characteristic polynomials from $EA-\lambda I$ and $A-\lambda I$ will generally not have the same roots. Consider the eigenvalues of these row equivalent matrices for example: $$ \begin{bmatrix} 2 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} \& \begin{bmatrix} 1 & 0 \\ 0 & 1 \end{bmatrix} $$
