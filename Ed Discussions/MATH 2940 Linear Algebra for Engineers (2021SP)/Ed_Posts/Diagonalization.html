Once we find the eigenvalues and check that the dimension is greater than or equal to the multiplicity can we simply state that the diagonalization of the matrix is the eigenvalues across the diagonal?

Author: Adrian Lewis (admin)

Vote Count: 0

The dimension can be smaller, but is never larger than the multiplicity, in fact. "Diagonalizing" a matrix means writing it in the form PDP^-1 where P is invertible and D is diagonal.
