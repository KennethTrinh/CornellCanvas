Author: Alma Thaler (student)

Vote Count: 3

because B-matrix of T is [(T(b1))b (T(b2))b ... ]. I think it's just the dimensions throwing you off. Since the B-matrix of T describes how T acts in B coordinates, you have to convert the answers you get from T(b1), TIb2), etc, which you called v1...v3, into B coordinates. B coordinate vectors only have 3 entries because the vector space spanned by B has 3 dimensions (there were 3 vectors in the given basis)
