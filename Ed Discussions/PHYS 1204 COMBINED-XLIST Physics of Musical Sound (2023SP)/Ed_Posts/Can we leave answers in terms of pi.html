Hi Nicolaas,

To help minimize calculation errors and maximize understanding, we like to do the following:

1. First, write out your answer with symbols / in equation form, without putting in numbers.

2. Second, write it out again with all the numbers - with units/dimensions - included.

3. Finally, give the final numeric answer with units, using an appropriate number of significant figures and powers of 10/scientific notation or standard unit prefixes (k=kilo, M=mega, m = milli, etc), using standard units (MKS in most cases in this course.)

4. Check that the units of the result match the units of the quantity to be found.

5. Check that the numeric answer is physically reasonable.

Scientists and engineers generally go through all these steps. If you cut any step out, you're more likely to miss any errors.

This can seem a bit arduous at first, but it eventually becomes automatic through practice.
