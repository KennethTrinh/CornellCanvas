To find the wave speed v, I found it easiest to simply express y(u) as a function of (x +/- vt), since we know that for any ideal transverse traveling wave, the displacement must be a function of this factor. Since we already have u in terms of x and t, simply rearrange it to fit that form and you should have a nice expression for wave speed.

Another way to say this is that we have a function which depends on $x$ and $t$ only through a combination of the form $k x + \omega t$ where $k$ and $\omega$ are constants. In this situation you can always conclude that the wave velocity is $v = \omega/k$.
