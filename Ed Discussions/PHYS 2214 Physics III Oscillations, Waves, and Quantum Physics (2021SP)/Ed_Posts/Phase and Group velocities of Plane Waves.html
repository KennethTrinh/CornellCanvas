A simple plane wave with one well defined wavelength will move at the phase velocity. The group velocity is really only relevant to analyze the superposition of two or more plane waves. If you know the dispersion curve $\omega(k)$ for a particular type of plane waves you can calculate the group velocity as $d\omega/dk$, but physically the group velocity only matters once you have a superposition. From your question, it sounds like you already have a good understanding of phase and group velocities.
