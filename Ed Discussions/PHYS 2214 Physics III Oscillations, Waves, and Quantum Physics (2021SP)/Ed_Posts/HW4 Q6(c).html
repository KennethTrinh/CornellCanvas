The way the expression is written is to make it clear what should happen in the zero case. \lim_{x\longrightarrow0}\ \frac{\sin x\ }{x}=1 This limit can be shown by L'Hopital's rule. Replace x with omega tau/2 in the limit expression to see that the same thing happens for the zero case of the Fourier transform.

Not meaning to disrespect hospitals, as a physicist you should understand this as a direct consequence of the Taylor series $\sin x = x - x^3/3!+\cdots$ (so $\sin x /x= 1 - x^2/3!+\cdots$).
