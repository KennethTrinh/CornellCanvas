I'll add one more remark to Kate's good answer. The e and o axes represent crystal planes transverse to the direction of light travel. We sometime draw them like a vector, but if you do that then you should keep in mind that the opposite direction of the same vector is also equivalent in depicting the e or o direction. That is, there is no +e and -e or +o and -o; there is no sign involved in specifying a particular crystal plane (The electric field in a wave is oscillatory, so saying the speed of the wave is different for one direction of electric field polarization versus a direction 180 degrees rotated makes no sense.) Because of this, as long as e is perpendicular to o then there is no sense in which one should speak of being rotated 90 degrees to the left versus being rotated 90 degrees to the right -- those are physically the same situations. It does make a difference, however, whether one particular optical axis is rotated 45 degrees clockwise or counterclockwise relative to the electric field polarization -- those are not equivalent configurations
