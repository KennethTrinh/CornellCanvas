There are at least a couple of approaches that will work. You can take the 2nd-order differential equation that you wrote down in part 7a and use the same mathematical approach that you used earlier in the lab: assume a complex valued charge $q(t) = q_0 e^{\alpha t}$ and find values of $\alpha$ that can satisfy the differential equation. Alternately, you could examine how the differential equation in part 7a corresponds to the mechanical-oscillator differential equation you solved earlier by renaming some of the parameters (e.g., the list of "equivalent variables" on page 2-12 of the Course Notes might be useful) and make use of your previous solution by just inserting the appropriate renaming of parameters.
