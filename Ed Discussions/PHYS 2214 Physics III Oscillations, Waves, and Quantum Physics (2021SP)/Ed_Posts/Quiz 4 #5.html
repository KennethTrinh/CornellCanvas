In Quiz 4 #5, how do we know that kinetic energy is proportional to μA^2? The equation for kinetic energy density has μ and dy/dt but what is the relationship between A and dy/dt?

----------- REPLIES -----------

Author: Dan Ralph (admin)

Vote Count: 0

For a standing wave we have that $y(x,t) = A sin(kx)cos(\omega t)$. The velocity is $dy/dt = - \omega A sin(kx) sin(\omega t)$. So at any instant the kinetic energy density is $\mu (dy/dt)^2 =\mu ( \omega A sin(kx) sin(\omega t))^2$. The maximum value of the kinetic energy density is therefore $ \mu ( \omega A)^2$.
