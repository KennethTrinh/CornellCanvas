This problem deals with an underdamped oscillator, so the general form of the position versus time is x\left(t\right)=Re\left[Ae^{i\ \phi}e^{-\frac{t}{\tau_A}}e^{i\omega t}\right]=Ae^{-\frac{t}{\tau_A}}\cos\left(\omega t+\phi\right) where here I have written a complex-valued A in terms of a real-valued A and a phase phi. When you take the time derivative of this to determine the velocity, there will be a contribution from taking the derivative of the e^{-\frac{t}{\tau_A}} term as well as the cosine. You can then use the initial conditions to determine A and phi.
