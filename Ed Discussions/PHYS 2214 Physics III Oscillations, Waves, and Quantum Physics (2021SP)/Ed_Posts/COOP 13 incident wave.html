The coop 13 wave is a function of (kz-wt). Expanded, it could be written Ecos(kz-wt)i+Ecos(kz-wt-pi/2). At z=0 this becomes Ecos(-wt)i+Ecos(-wt-pi/2)j. Because cosine is an 'even' function, this is the same as Ecos(wt)i+Ecos(wt+pi/2)j. Note that the y term actually has a +pi/2 phase--it is ahead, not behind.

The signs were changed in my explanation only to show consistency with the equation shown in the text. That equation assumes +z propagation. What ends up missing from the text equation you showed, is the direction of propagation (which is +z).

The direction of propagation does matter. Clockwise circulation in the x-y plane would be right-hand circular polarization for +z propagation, but left-hand circular polarization for -z propagation. Simply adding a phase of -pi/2 to the y-component of the electric field gives different circular polarizations for different propagation directions. Ecos(-wt)i+Ecos(-wt-pi/2)j is different from Ecos(wt)i+Ecos(wt-pi/2)j.
