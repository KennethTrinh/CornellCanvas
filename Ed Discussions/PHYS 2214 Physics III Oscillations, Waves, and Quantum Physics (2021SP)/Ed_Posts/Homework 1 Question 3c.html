I like this question, too! I think in a careful treatment there will actually be a continuous crossover for the decay times between the underdamped and overdamped regimes. The expressions given in the Course Notes for the decay times in the overdamped regime are derived assuming that $b^2 \gg 4mk$ -- that the system is $very$ overdamped (see the discussion leading up to Equations 1-37 and 1-38). This assumption does not hold if you imagine starting in the highly damped regime and reducing $b$ until you cross over to the underdamped regime. It is, however, also possible to calculate the two decay times in the overdamped regime without approximation. The answers are \tau_1=\frac{2m}{b-\sqrt{b^2-4mk}};\ \tau_2=\frac{2m}{b+\sqrt{b^2-4mk}}. From this you can see that if you reduce $b$ until $b^2 = 4mk$ that the decay constants converge to $2m/b$, the decay constant in the underdamped regime.
