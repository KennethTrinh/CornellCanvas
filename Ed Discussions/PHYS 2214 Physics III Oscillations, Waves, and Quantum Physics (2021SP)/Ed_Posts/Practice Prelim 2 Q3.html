The half-wave plate changes the incident linear polarization direction by 'reflecting' that orientation across the plate's optical axis. For example, if the axis is at 5 degrees, the original 0 degree polarization is transmitted at 10 degrees. The 'swing' of the polarization orientation is 2x(5 degrees)--from 0 to 10.
