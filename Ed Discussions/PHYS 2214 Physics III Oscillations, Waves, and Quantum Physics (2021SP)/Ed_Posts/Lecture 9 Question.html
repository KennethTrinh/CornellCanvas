(delta omega)x(delta t)=1 applies to a Gaussian pulse. The Gaussian pulse is the special case for which (delta omega)x(delta t) has its smallest value. Mathematically, the product '1' relies upon "delta" being defined as the standard deviation of the pulse shape function. In this slide, we are using "delta" rather casually--it is not the rigorous standard deviation. Nonetheless, the qualitative idea of a narrow pulse shape resulting in a broader Fourier spectrum is still illustrated.
