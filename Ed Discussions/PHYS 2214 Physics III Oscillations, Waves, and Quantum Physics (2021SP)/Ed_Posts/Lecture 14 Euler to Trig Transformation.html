I think instead of getting rid of the imaginary part, try multiplying by its complex conjugate. so \left(1\ +\ \cos\left(\theta\right)\ +\ i\sin\left(\theta\right)\right)\ \cdot\ \left(1\ +\ \cos\left(\theta\right)\ -\ i\sin\left(\theta\right)\right) And then take the square root to get: \sqrt{2+2\cos\left(\theta\right)}=\ \left|2\cos\left(\frac{\theta}{2}\right)\right| (I asked about this in office hours)
