The angular direction of the principal maximum depends upon the phase imposed. If the phase is zero, the angle is zero--perpendicular to the line along which the sources lie. For a non-zero phase the angular direction will be some angle other than zero. Suppose you had a phase 'knob' to vary the phase. What range of phase choices would result in being able to choose any angular direction of your choice between -90 and +90 degrees?
