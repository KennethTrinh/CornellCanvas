Let's start by determining the position of the intensity zeros. From thinking about phasor diagrams, one can see that for $N$ coherent sources the zeros occur when the phase difference due to the different path lengths associated with neighboring sources is $\Delta \phi = 2 \pi/N$. (This corresponds to $N-1$ zeros in between principal maxima.) For an arbitrary angle $\theta$ traveling away from the sources, the phase difference between adjacent paths is $\Delta \phi = k d sin\theta$. The zeros of intensity therefore correspond to $k d sin\theta = 2 \pi/N$ or $ sin\theta = \lambda/(dN)$ (since $k = 2 \pi/\lambda$), and the difference between the two neighboring zeros next to the principal maximum is twice this value.

The symbol $\theta$ corresponds to the angle at which the wave travels to reach a certain point on the screen and the symbol $\Delta \theta$ corresponds to the difference in this angle between two intensity zeros on either side of a principal maximum.
