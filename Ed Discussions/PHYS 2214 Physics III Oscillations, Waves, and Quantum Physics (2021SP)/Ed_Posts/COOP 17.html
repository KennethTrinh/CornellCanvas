There are two cases outlined in the course notes: if we have three materials, with light going from 1 to 2 to 3 and reflecting at the 1-2 boundary and 2-3 boundary, then it matters what the refractive indices are of each material.

Let's assume $n_1<n_2$. If $n_2<n_3$ there is a $\pi$ phase change at both of the two boundaries, so Al is correct. However, if $n_2>n_3$ there is a $\pi$ phase change at only the 1-2 boundary, and Anon would be correct.

The first case is equations 16-6 and 16-7; the second is 16-8 and 16-9.
