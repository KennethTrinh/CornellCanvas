In the reading, we have that

I\left(\ \theta\right)=I_{1\ slit}\left(\theta\ \right)\times I_{N\ sources}\left(\theta\right)

Just to make sure,

I_{1\ slit}=I_o\frac{\sin^2\left(\frac{ka}{2}\sin\left(\theta\right)\right)}{\left(\frac{ka}{2}\sin\left(\theta\right)\right)^{2\ }}

and

I_{N\ sources}=I_{o\ }\left(\frac{\sin^2\left(\frac{Nkd\sin\left(\theta\right)}{2}\right)}{\sin^2\left(\frac{kd\sin\left(\theta\right)}{2}\right)}\right)

So the total intensity is just the product of these two?

Author: Dan Ralph (admin)

Vote Count: 1

Yes, for the case of $N$ equally-spaced slits with spacing $d$ each with width $a$, if they are illuminated by a single wavelength of light with no initial phase difference between the different slits.

Strictly speaking, you are right; two intensities can't be multiplied together. It is the dimensionless factors following the I_o coefficient that are multiplied together to get the net fractional factor applied to the intensity.
