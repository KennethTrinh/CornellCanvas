Today (May 1st) is a nice and sunny day but I've tried this experiment with two different pairs of sunglasses and I do not notice any difference when turning my head looking perpendicular to the sun's rays. I was under the impression that all non-reflective sunglasses are polarized; does this mean that not all sunglasses are polarized? And in this case, should I just consult another classmate who did notice something (and properly credit them, of course)?

Author: Andreea Foarce (student)

Vote Count: 2

An easy way to check if they are polarized would be to hold them up to your computer screen and see if you notice a difference at different angles (it should black out the screen at one angle if it is polarized). Not all sunglasses are polarized, it is a chemical treatment done to the lenses that usually increases the price of the glasses, so a lot of sunglasses are not polarized (just tinted).
