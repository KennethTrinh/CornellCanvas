I'm not sure precisely what you are asking, but I can help you get started here. To calculate the probability distribution function at $x=L/4$, what you want to do is multiply $\Psi(x,t)$ by the complex conjugate of $\Psi(x,t)$ and evaluate at $x=L/4$. After some algebra, this will give you a real-valued oscillatory function of time for the probability density at this position.
