The angular position of the first zero next to the principal maximum at $\theta = 0$ would occur at an angle $\theta_{min}$ determined by the formula $d sin(\theta_{min}) = 1 \lambda/8$, where $N = 8$ is the number of sources. From this you could solve for $\theta_{min}$ and then the full width of the principal maximum would be 2 $\theta_{min}$. The answer given in the solution is approximately equal to this when $N$ is large enough that the angle $\theta_{min}$ is small, but strictly speaking I think it is more correct to solve for 2$\theta_{min}$ rather than $\Delta \theta$.
