The idea leading to 8-25 is that the displacements of both strings at the boundary must be the same so that the string doesn't break (have a gap). Similarly, the displacements of both fluids at the boundary must be the same so that there is no interval of vacuum (gap) or of two fluids simultaneously occupying the same volume. So yes, 8-25 is still applicable to the fluid situation.

You have correctly identified that Course Notes page 8-7 are relevant and useful as a model. For strings, the force of each string at the junction has magnitude (tau)(dy/dx). For the columns of fluids, the force of each fluid at the junction is (pressure)x(area) and pressure is related to displacement by P = -B(ds/dx). So the forces in both situations depends upon the 'slope.' The vital step you may be missing is that strings must have the same tension (tau), but fluids will have different bulk moduli. So while in Course Notes equation 8-26 all equal values of tau factor out of the relation, that won't be the case for bulk moduli in the case of fluids.
