Given that maxima is the first maxima from the central one we know that n=1. The wavelength is 187.5m. Then we plug in those knowns into the equation [d * sin(theta) = n * wavelength]. The principal maxima are 2 times wider than the spacing between minima when plotted as a function of sin($\theta$), not when plotted as a function of $\theta$. For small angles you can make the approximation that sin($\theta$) $\approx \theta$, but this is not true when you consider larger angles. In this problem, there is no value of $\theta$ that will give you a $n=1$ principal maximum, because this would require a value of sin($\theta$) greater than one.

You would get the correct answer using the formula for the 4th zero of the diffraction pattern for 5 sources:

$d sin($90 degrees$) = 4 \lambda/5$, and solving for $d$, but it is generally easier (and less likely to lead to mistakes) if you analyze these types of problems in terms of phasors.
