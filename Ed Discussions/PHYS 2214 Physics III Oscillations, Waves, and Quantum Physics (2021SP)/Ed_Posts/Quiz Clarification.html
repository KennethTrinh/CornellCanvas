Each fan follows the motion of their -x neighbor in both the upward and downward sweeps. I don't believe the solution depends on which way you visualize the problem (either Downward 1 or Downward 2) as long as $\theta$ goes up during the upward sweeps and down during the downward sweeps. However, typically the wave is done with arms tilted upward and downward in the forward direction, rather than to the side, so your picture of Downward 2 is the more physically accurate.
