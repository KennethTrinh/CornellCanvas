Here is an image that might help you picture light rays emerging from one point at the focal plane of a convex and refracting to travel parallel to each other after passing through the convex lens. (Think of the light traveling from right to left.) Here is the corresponding image for a concave lens. In order for light rays to emerge parallel to each other after passing through the concave lens, they must start out incident from a wide range of different angles. There is no situation for which light rays emerging from one point will pass through a concave lens and then be bent to travel parallel to each other. Since light rays will follow the same paths going through a lens either left-to-right or right-to-left, you can also understand what is going on in these sorts of problems by imagining that you start with a set of light rays traveling parallel to each other and ask whether they bend toward each other or away from each other upon passing through the lens.
