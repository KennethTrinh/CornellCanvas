Your dz/dt derivative is correct. Even though there is now an 'i' in the coefficient, the complex exponential still has real and imaginary parts. Multiplying those read and imaginary parts by 'i' gives you an imaginary and a real part: [i*i sin(theta)] becomes the real part.
