These equations are OK for electrons. For photons, it is true that $p = h/\lambda$, but not $p = mv$, because the mass of a photon is zero but it can still have nonzero momentum.

The equation that does not hold for electrons is $v = \omega/k$, because particles move at the group velocity, not the phase velocity.
