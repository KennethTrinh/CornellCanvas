What does s subscript m and p subscript m mean?

----------- REPLIES -----------

Author: Dan Ralph (admin)

Vote Count: 1

$s_m$ and $p_m$ are used to denote the maximum amplitudes of a harmonically-oscillating sound wave. The Course notes also use $s_0$ and $p_0$ for the same quantity. That is $s(x,t) = s_m cos(kx \mp \omega t +\phi)$ and $p(x,t) = p_m sin(kx \mp \omega t +\phi)$.
