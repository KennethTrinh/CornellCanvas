With triangular shapes, there is no defined slope or derivative at the peak. We ignore this discontinuity point, but can still get slopes and energies from all other points on the string. The key here is that the slopes of the overlapping pulses are identical, so the overlapping slope doubles. The string velocities, however, are opposite (different algebraic signs in the pulse equations for different propagation directions); the overlapping velocities sum to zero. Remember to square the derivatives after superposition (adding them together); it is incorrect to square the pulse 1 and pulse 2 derivatives prior to adding them.

The partial with respect to x is simply the slope. The partial with respect to t can be found by using the "pulse equation": dy/dt = v (dy/dx) or dy/dt = -v (dy/dx).
