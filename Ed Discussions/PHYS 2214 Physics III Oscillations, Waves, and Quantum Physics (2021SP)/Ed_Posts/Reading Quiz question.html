Author: Dan Ralph (admin)

Vote Count: 0

If you have an object moving past the position $y = 0$ at some nonzero velocity, you have $dy/dt \neq 0$ even though $y = 0$. So having zero displacement at some particular instance of time does not mean that the displacement is zero at all times or that there is zero velocity.

From your original question, "If the y displacement is 0 then doesnt that mean delta y = 0." Answer: emphatically "no!" The condition y=0 does not imply delta-y=0.
