The difference between regions 1 and 3 is that region 3 just has a transmitted wave moving in the +x direction (including the time dependence, the full wavefunction in region 3 is $\psi_3(x,t)= F e^{i(k_1 x - \omega t)}$), while region 1 has both an incident wave moving in the +x direction ($A e^{i(k_1 x - \omega t)}$) plus a reflected wave moving in the -x direction ($B e^{i(-k_1 x - \omega t)}$). If you take the wavefunction in region 3 and multiply it by its complex conjugate, you get a result that does not depend on either position (within region 3) or time. However, when you take the full wavefunction in region 1 and multiply by its complex conjugate, the result is not independent of position. The full algebra for region 1 is worked out explicitly on slide 13 of Lecture 25.
