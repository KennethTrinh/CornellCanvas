If a linearly polarized wave is passed through a linear polarizer whose polarization axis is pi + theta, where theta is the incident wave's polarization angle, the polarizer would not allow anything through. Is this correct?

----------- REPLIES -----------

Author: Dan Ralph (admin)

Vote Count: 0

The wave is blocked if the transmission axis of the polarizer is rotated by $\pm \pi/2$ relative to the polarization angle of the wave, not by $\pi$. There is no change upon rotating the transmission axis by $\pi$.
