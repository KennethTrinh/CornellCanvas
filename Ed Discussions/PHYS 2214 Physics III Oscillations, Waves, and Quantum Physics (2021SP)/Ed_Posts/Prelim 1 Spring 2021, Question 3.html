For part (a): The time scale over which oscillator 1 reaches its full oscillation amplitude is longer than for oscillator 2. This time scale is $\tau_A = 2Q_0/\omega_0$. Since the resonant frequency $\omega_0$ is the same for both oscillators, this means that the quality factor is larger for oscillator 1. (Higher Q means lower damping and for an underdamped oscillator lower damping means that the time to reach equilibrium is longer.) For part (b). For a given drive amplitude, the higher Q oscillator will have a larger oscillation amplitude when driven at the resonance frequency. Since in this case the two oscillators have the same steady-state oscillation amplitude, that means that the drive amplitude for oscillator 2 (the one with lower Q) must be larger than for oscillator 1.
