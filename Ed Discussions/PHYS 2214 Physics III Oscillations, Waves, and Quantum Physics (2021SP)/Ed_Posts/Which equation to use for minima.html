The two equations you reference say the same thing, but from a different perspective. "Phi" is a phase parameter that can be thought of as a 'normalized' geometric angle "theta." So phi = 2(pi) refers to a complete 'cycle' of the interference pattern from one principal maximum to the next principal maximum. "Theta" is a geometric angle at which some feature (minimum or maximum) occurs. The theta difference from one principal maximum to the next principal maximum will be much less than 2(pi).
