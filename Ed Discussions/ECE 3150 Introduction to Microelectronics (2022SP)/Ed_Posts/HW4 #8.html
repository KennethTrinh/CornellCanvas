Author: Max McCarthy (staff)

You don't have to worry about that. Usually, $V_D$ represents the voltage at the Drain with respect to GND. Drain current, $I_D$, is really a function of $V_{GS}$ and/or $V_{DS}$—which can be used to find expressions for $g_m$ and $\frac{1}{r_o}$ respectively—by taking the partial derivatives of $I_D$.
