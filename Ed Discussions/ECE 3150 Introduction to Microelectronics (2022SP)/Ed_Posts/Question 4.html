Hi, as I explained in the previous thread, the signs indicate the direction of currents. If the electron and hole diffusion currents flow to the same direction, they add up, and if not, you need to subtract.

==============================

The sign of the current density term reflects the current flowing direction. Normally if the current flows from left to right in x-axis, it has a positive sign.

In the above case, the electron diffusion current is

J_{e,diff}=qD_n\frac{dn}{dx}

and the hole diffusion current is

J_{h,diff}=-qD_p\frac{dp}{dx}

The figure is from Neamen, Donald A. Semiconductor physics and devices : basic principles Donald A. Neamen. - 4rd ed., McGraw-Hill, ISBN 0-07-232107-5
