Author: Kirsten Scheller (staff)

Vote Count: 1

Unlike the first two circuits, there is no current source on the base. In the simple circuit given here there no current shown to be going into the base. BJTs are current-controlled current sources. Intuitively, it may be easier to think about the base-emitter junction as a sort of diode. When there is no current into the base, the base is pulled down to the emitter. This is what is happening here with the base left "floating" and there is no current flow into the base. Hopefully this explanation is helpful.
