For the purpose of this question, where the goal is to write the full equation for each diode voltage, they are not equal. You can write an equation for Vg in terms of Vd1 and Vd2. Then you can solve for either one first, and when you plug in to calculate the other, you will see they are not the same final equation.

Author: Kirsten Scheller (staff)

Vote Count: 2

Yes, KVL can help find the diode voltages. The currents going through the diode (little i) are the same because they are in the same current branch. What is different is the saturation current (big I) used in the diode model equation. Since you know the same current i is going through both diodes, you can solve for i by writing the diode current equation for one diode. Then, you would have to plug in your equations for Vd1 and Vd2 into the i equation. You can simplify this combined equation to get i in terms of Vg, Vt, Is1 and Is2
