For forward active regime it is true that:

Vc > Vb and Vb > Ve.

On HW2, Q3, it says that Vbe = 0.7V, yet the Vce sat = 0.2V. I was wondering how this was possible because Ve is grounded, so Ve = 0. Then since Vbe = 0.7V and Ve = 0V, Vb = 0.7V. So, to be in forward active Vc > Vb, so Vc > 0.7V must be true.

But, the problem says Vce sat = 0.2V and since Ve = 0V, Vc > 0.2V must be true. Again, given the conditions of forward active, I thought Vc > 0.7V would have to be true.

How is this possible?

V CE sat is a device parameter. However, V BE is where we are currently operating. At this operating point, V CE isn't equal to V CE sat (as you'd be right - we wouldn't be in forward active!)

Going a bit further:

3a) V BE is 0.7 for this question . This would correspond to a V CE >0.7V

3b) Now we're trying to find the limit of being in forward active. Here, the minimum is in the case where V CE =V CE sat . At this operating point, V BE would have to be less than 0.2V to be in forward active
