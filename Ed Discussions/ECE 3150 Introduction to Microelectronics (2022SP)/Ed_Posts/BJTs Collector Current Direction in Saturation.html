When we drew a general structural diagram of an npn BJT, we defined the collector current to be flowing into the collector terminal. When an npn BJT is in saturation mode, however, the base-collector junction is in forward bias, which I think from my understanding would indicate that current flows from the base to the collector. Does this mean that I_c is actually flowing out of the collector terminal, in the opposite direction to our convention for npn's, when the BJT is in saturation mode?

Author: Clifford Pollock (admin)

Vote Count: 2

The current flows into the collector until the collector-emitter voltage get to nearly zero. Think about your lab this week, you measured the collector current and found it started at zero when the V_ce=0, and grew steadily until it reached the active region at V_ce about 1/2 V. The built-in reverse bias of the Base-collector junction continues to sweep up minority carriers even when the base-collector voltage is weak, but still reverse. It stays reverse until the base-collector voltage reaches about 0.5 volts, From the base current perspective, there probably is a small fraction of base current that goes toward the collector as that reverse bias decreases, but the total current entering the collector is still dominant.
