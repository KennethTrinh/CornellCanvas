The three forms of gm for a mosfet you can pretty much use anytime: the output impedance ro is really only valid during saturation because in triode, we evaluate an on resistance R_on separately. The equations are very similar. The only difference is that ro has the additional lambda (channel length modulation parameter) factor in the denominator. Since lambda is less than 1, this makes ro larger and tending towards infinity (which is the ideal case when there is no channel length modulation). The version of the expression r_o\approx\frac{1}{\lambda I_D} is valid only for "small" lambda (let's say approximately 0.1 or around there). All of the above expressions are for MOSFETs only. ______________ For BJTs only, you have a different set of expressions g_m=\frac{I_c}{V_T} and r_o=\frac{V_A}{I_c}
