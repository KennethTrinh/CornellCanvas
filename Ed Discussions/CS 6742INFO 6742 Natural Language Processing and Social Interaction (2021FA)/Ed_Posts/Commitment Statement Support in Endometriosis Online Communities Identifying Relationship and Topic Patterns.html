    Since writing this commitment statement, I began by setting up Prodigy for the three different labeling tasks: relationships, subjects, and intent. Now, Fede and I can easily switch between labeling schemes, add new labels, and access/merge the labeled data. I updated some instructions that I previously created on how to set up Prodigy based on a machine, so I also have instructions that could be shared easily if we want to get anyone else set up with labeling.

    I began by labeling the relationships category, with the following six categories: family, friend, partner, doctor, therapist, and endo support community. If the relationships category is mentioned in a post, then I label the post as having that relationship in it. This means that there are many posts with multiple labels (especially endo support community and doctors) and some without any of the relationship categories--though that is rare. The most common category is, perhaps unsurprisingly, the endo support community. Many posts begin or end with a note directed toward the community, either asking them for support, experiences, or information or stating that they hope to help other people in the community by sharing.

    I did notice a few categories that seemed to be missing. One (very interesting) type of post occurs when the author of the post doesn't have endo; they post to the community to see how they can help someone they are closely connected to with endo. I added the "non-endo-author" category so that we can catch these moments, since it feels very important if we are curious about how people discuss, experience, and mediate their relationships and find support for endo. I also added an "other" category for moments where the OP discusses coworkers, pets, etc. since these are still relationships, albeit uncommonly referenced. So far, I have labeled over 200 posts, and have found the relationship category fairly easy to identify and label.

    Fede and I met last week and discussed changing the labels she uses for the topics, given that we began by only considering symptoms. We agreed to add a few more labels to that category, and that I would try labeling some of the topics category since it's more difficult to identify in comparison to the relationships category.
