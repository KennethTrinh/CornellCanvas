This project aims to learn lexical innovation in the online community. I am especially interested in the following questions:

  • What’s the lexical innovation in the given community

  • New words dissemination pattern

  • What kind of word will be widely accepted by the community

    • Defining what’s lexical innovation would be the very first step.

  • For the word never used before, used >=10 times by multiple users talking about different products for a period of 6 months after first use. [1]

  • Identify all words that were not used during the first years of the group, and that was consistently used for at least some years thereafter. No more than 20% of occurrences in a single month, no more than 40% of occurrences by a single user, and no more than 80% of occurrences by five users. [2]

    • For the word dissemination pattern, I plan to calculate the dissemination of words across words and threads using formulas defined in [3] and draw the graph similar the Figure 1 in [4], but not represented in the geographical dissemination(maybe in community dissemination).

    Hypothesis 1: Different types of words will have different dissemination patterns. For example P words (products and public figures) vs S words (slang words) [3]

    Hypothesis 2: Lexical innovation of highly related communities evolves together.

    Inspired by [3], I am going to observe lexical innovation of highly related communities such as Science, AskScience, and BadScience. I am going to test prevalence correlation and co-occurrence[5] of the same words among highly related communities.

    For what kind of words will be widely accepted, I am making the following assumptions:

    Hypothesis1: Easily accessible words are more likely to be widely accepted: number of phonemes, length of the word

    Hypothesis2: If the word is mentioned by an authoritative expert in the field, it’s more likely to be spread.

    Hypothesis3: Different communities and topics will have very different preferences towards words.

    Feasibility Check:

  • Tuning with different lexical innovation definitions on different communities, and check if there are enough innovation words to study question 3. Make sure to select the Reddit group with great lexical creativity.

  • Observe if the same new words appear in the highly related communities and analyze the prevalence correlation and co-occurrence.

    Because the original file of the whole Reddit dataset is too large, I used the subreddit in the Convokit instead. Although it’s much easier to use, it misses some earlier conversations on Reddit.

    I tested two ways to define lexical innovation on the three sub-communities: BadScience, AskScience, Wine with different thresholds.

  • For the word never used before, used >=10 times by multiple users talking about different products for a period of 6 months after first use. [1]

  • Identify all words that were not used during the first years of the group, and that was consistently used for at least some years thereafter. No more than 20% of occurrences in a single month, no more than 40% of occurrences by a single user, and no more than 80% of occurrences by five users. [2]

    • Initial Observation:

  • Posts are very limited at the start of the subcommunity and surge at a certain point

  • Vocabulary size is steadily increasing for different communities

  • There are more innovative words in the bigger sub-community

  • By using the lexical innovation definition [1], we get a much smaller set of lexical innovations. And innovation definition [2] creates too many lexical innovations.

  • Threshold greatly influences the number of lexical innovations found.

    • There are some words that are regarded as lexical innovation by definition [2] by not definition [1]. For example “realism”. Although it’s not used before 2014/07. It’s used less than 10 times in the beginning when the word was brought up and caused a huge debate in the future.

    For the lexical innovation defined by [1], there are two very common patterns.

    Users of the BadScience community started to cite ‘/r/snapshillbot’ subreddit broadly from 2016/06. And only talks about ‘ebola’ in a very short break because of the outbreak of the disease.
