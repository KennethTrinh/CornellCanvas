    I’m curious about the role of verbal diversity. The type/token ratio (TTR) is defined as the number of unique words/number of words. How is a unique word defined? We were taught to use an assortment of words in debate but to avoid jargon since that may confuse the audience. The audience is unknown in an online discussion, but I wonder if the same idea still applies. Given the conclusion, the next step would be to explore the effect of a broad vocabulary. The possible procedure could be:

  • Set a range for comment length

  • For each original post, find a comment within the range that convinced OP and one that did not

  • Excluding stop words, determine verbal diversity using TTR

    I think here "unique words" just means "words if you don't count repetitions". So, the TTR for "the cat the bat the mat" is 4/6: four unique words ("the", "cat", "bat", "mat") divided by six words if repeats are counted.
