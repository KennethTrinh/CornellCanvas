  • Sentiment

  • Check the utterances with the lowest/highest score for Vader

  • with higher 'compound' score -> should be more positive

  • with lower 'compound' score -> should be more negative

  • In general, for the "extreme" cases, the classifier works well. Those with high scores are indeed very positive utterances, and those with low scores are with negative emotions. However, there are still several problems:

  • When looking at these examples and begining labeling, I start thinking about the problem "what utterance should be positive/neutral/negative". As far as I could see, there are two definitions, one for "show signals for agreement or support the utterances before", another one is for "emotionally positive or negative". The case for neutral is even more tricky, if one is stating some facts but are actually including some sad story - if a persuader is telling how the children in poor countries are in need of food or water, intuitionally it should be negative. However, it is not some "negative" feedback, but just stating some facts, should we still consider it as negative or just neutral? Also, if one asks me to do something that I do not really want to do but I still agree to do so, I reply with "well I could do that", which is positive feedback but in fact with some "negative" emotion, should I take it as positive or neutral, or even negative? I will need to clarify this problem before I could really move forward. For now, I am more on the side of using the "emotional" definition, but could also try to do both, as I believe that either emotionally positive or attitude-wise positive should have some correlation with the final successful persuasion.

  • It is also notable that utterances are always comparatively long if with a higher absolute score - it might also introduce bias, and simply normalize by the length of utterances might be risky.

  • An extra problem is that for this persuasion dataset, upon my observations, fairly many utterances are "neutral", but currently I am not sure if Vader is trustworthy enough on those utterances.
