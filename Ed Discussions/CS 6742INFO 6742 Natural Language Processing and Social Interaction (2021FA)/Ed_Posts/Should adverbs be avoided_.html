Title: Should adverbs be avoided?

Author: Benjamin Rosche (student)

Date: 2021-09-08T02:34:54.969453+10:00

Category: A1

Vote Count: 0

Books on how to write persuasively (e.g., Sword 2012) often note that the use of adverbs should be avoided. Adverbs have a reputation for being weak and causing unnecessary clutter. This is because plenty of adverb-verb combinations are often best expressed with a single, more meaningful verb. For example, "driving fast” could become “speeding”, "walking slowly" could become "meandering", etc. I wonder whether such writing tips hold up against data. To test the hypothesis, I propose to use the CMV dataset and test whether the frequency of adverb use is negatively correlated with persuasiveness. I found a website with roughly 4000 adverbs, which I could use to identify adverbs. This project could be merged into Kiran's or Remy's project as the testing procedure might be fairly similar.
