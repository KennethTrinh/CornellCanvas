Totally forgot to say that I posted the following paper to the lecture website for today

  • Liao, Jingxian, Guowei Yang, David Kavaler, Vladimir Filkov, and Prem Devanbu. 2019. Status, Identity, and Language: A Study of Issue Discussions in GitHub . PLOS ONE14 (6): e0215059.

    • because it shows an interesting example --- for those of you who might be thinking of using the most modern deep-learning methods for your projects, but don't see how it's amenable to hypothesis-driven research, of:

  • using deep learning, often thought of as not amenable for understanding feature contributions (since the representation is learned, not supplied), for classification, but

  • then uses an ablation methodology to use the deep learning architecture to investigate particular features of interest.

    • So, for example, they give classification accuracy, but then, to investigate the effect of closed- vs open-category words, they replace all the open-category words with a special single token and re-run the classification.
