The default constructor is only inserted when a constructor is defined....

Super is inputted automatically with a default constructor

So why if there is a constructor implemented (that is not the default constructor) does initialization have to be top down?

Author: David Gries (admin)

Vote Count: 1

1. Every constructor you write must begin with either

this(...); or super(...);

Constructors cannot be recursive.

Therefore, at least one constructor will have a call. super(...); at its beginning.

2. The principle "Fill in superclass fields first" is the proper OO way to fill in fields.
