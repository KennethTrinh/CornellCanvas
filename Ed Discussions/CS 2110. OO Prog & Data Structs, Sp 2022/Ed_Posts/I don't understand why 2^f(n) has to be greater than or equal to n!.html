Author: Sasha Loayza (student)

Basically, when making a series of comparisons, you are trying to figure out which one of the n! permutations of an array of size n is the actual one you are looking at. Since f(n), which is just a placeholder for "the maximum number of comparisons to identify a list of size $n$", cannot distinguish one array from more than 2^f(n) other arrays (permutations), that number must be at least as big n!, which is how many permutations actually have to be distinguished. In terms of merge sort, at least according to symbolab, the limit of $\frac{2^{n log(n)}}{n!}$ is infinity, so it seems to work out.
