For the loop condition B, why is k>=m not correct? Why should it be k>m?

----------- REPLIES -----------

Author: Vladia Trinh (staff)

Vote Count: 1

Think about the case when k == m. At that point, s = product of these for b[m..n] according to the loop invariant. If that is the case, we are done because that is precisely the post condition. So the condition should be k > m or k != m, because at the iteration where k == m, we meet the post condition and should terminate, not execute the loop body.
