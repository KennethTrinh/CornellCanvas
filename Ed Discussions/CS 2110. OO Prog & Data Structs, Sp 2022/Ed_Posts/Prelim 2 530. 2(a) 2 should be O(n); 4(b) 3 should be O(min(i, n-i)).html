My guess is that the translation of O(i) to O(n) regarding the inner loop's time complexity comes from the fact that the inner loop will run at most n times during the last iteration of the outer loop (i.e. when i = n, in the worst case).

I believe the documentation you are referencing simply states that all methods defined in class LinkedList<E> perform similarly when used by either singly-linked or doubly-linked lists. Given that course staff instructed us to assume any LinkedLists to be singly-linked unless stated otherwise, the complication of doubly-linked traversal becomes irrelevant in the context of the question.
