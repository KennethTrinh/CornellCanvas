Hi, no worries about potentially repeat questions! Same deal as Prelim 1: we expect you to know common operations, and will give you the specifications for anything we wouldn't expect you to memorize. I am probably forgetting something major and embarrassing, but for now I can't remember anything new since prelim 1 except the following, which you should know: * method calls: use the form <object>.<method_name>(args), not <classname>.<method_name>(<object>, <args>) * dictionary syntax: reference an entry, loop through the entries. days = {"Monday": "is fair of face", "Tuesday": "is full of grace", "Wednesday": "is full of woe", "Thursday": "has far to go"} days["Thursday"] = "... wait, that's prelim day, bro!" for day in days: print(day + "'s child " + days[day])
