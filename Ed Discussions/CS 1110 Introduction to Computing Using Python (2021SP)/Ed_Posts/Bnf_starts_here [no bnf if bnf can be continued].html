Formal definition of bnf, section 7.1.1., says that for k=1, it has to be that "there is no reply to P written by u2. (This includes the case where P has no replies at all)" Or, as the rewording at footnote 14 says, "Contrariwise, no back-and-forth of length k = 1 between u1 and u2 starts at P if P wasn’t written by u1 or if there’s a reply to P written by u2."
