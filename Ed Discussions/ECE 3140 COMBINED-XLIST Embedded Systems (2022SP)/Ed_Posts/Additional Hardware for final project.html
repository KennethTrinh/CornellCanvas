For a motor, you should not try to directly hook it up to the boards output pins since they will not be able to delivery enough current. Trying to draw such a current would fry the pin, port, or possibly entire board. A motor should be hooked up using an H-bridge and an external power supply. You can then supply the appropriate signals to the H-bridge to spin the motor.

For something like a servo, they usually have pins for power, ground, and control. Power and ground can be hooked up to an external supply, and the control pin can be connected to a pin on the KL46Z set up for PWM.
