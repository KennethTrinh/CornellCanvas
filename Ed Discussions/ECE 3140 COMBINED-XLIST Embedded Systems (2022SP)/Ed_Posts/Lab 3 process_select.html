The variable current_process is never modified by the support code and will only operate on the stack pointers returned by process_select . When a process finishes executing, you will know this as cursp will be NULL . Then you may take appropriate action to nullify the current_process . Correct. I do not understand your example. Switching back and forth is the same as round-robin when you only have two processes. For a larger number of processes, you will be using round-robin and switching to a different one at each context switch. Here is an example where you have three processes where process 1 runs indefinitely, process 2 finishes in its 2nd time slot, and process 3 finishes in its 4th time slot. 1, 2, 3, 1, 2, 3, 1, 3, 1, 3, 1, 1, 1,...

You cannot leave the terminated process. It contains malloc 'd memory which must be free 'd to avoid memory leaks. Your proposition still supports concurrency, but lacks fairness between all processes. We want to ensure that all ready processes are equally advancing in their work.
