You shouldn't have to recreate a new workspace every time you open the IDE -- something is not working quite right. My guess is that there is a ghost process of MCUXpresso (left behind when you last opened the program) that is still hogging the workspace in the background even though it is not visible.

First, make sure you close out of MCUXpresso before you restart or turn off you computer. If the problem is happening and you're on Windows then open up the Task Manager and terminate MCUXpresso if you see it. That should end the process and hopefully relinquish the workspace, allowing you to work on it
