You can , but a couple things arise:

  • At least when I tried it, I got linker errors of multiple definitions (it was defining the enqueue function twice somewhere), even with pre-compiler #ifndef statements (yea, it was weird)

  • You actually want your enqueue functions to be slightly different for the process queue and locks! (Think about what the next_process pointer should point to)

    • Because of this, it's probably easier to just define a new function (enqueue_lock) inside of lock.c, and use that for enqueuing to lists of blocked processes
