This is correct. You can poll PIT1 similar to how we did in Lab2, and on the event that PIT1 finishes you can call your PIT1_Service . Interrupts need not be modified for this method to work. If nRT processes are available and RT processes are not ready (or do not exist), you must run the nRT processes. If no nRT processes are left and RT processes are not ready, you must wait for one to become ready. Otherwise you would be terminating execution with incomplete and un-free'd processes left in the queues. The only condition for terminating your scheduler is if there are no processes left anywhere.
