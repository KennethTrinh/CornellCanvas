No question is "dumb"! All questions rule! Harmonically related means that the ratio of any two of them is a rational number . That's restrictive. For example, $f _ 1 = 1$ and $f _ 2 = \sqrt{2}$ aren't harmonically related frequencies, and the signal $x ( t ) = \cos ( 2 \pi t ) + \cos ( 2 \pi ( \sqrt{2} ) t )$ isn't periodic. It has nothing to do with $N$, or the number of terms in the summation, or whatever. It has to do with whether the individual cosine terms are "in sync" with each other. (Or is it "in synch"?)
