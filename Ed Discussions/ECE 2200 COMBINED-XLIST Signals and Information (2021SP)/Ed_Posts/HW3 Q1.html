I get stuck when I plug in values for k and try to translate them into plots on the frequency spectrum. For example, when I plug in k=0, I get just the sine term: 1/2j * exp(j 24pi t) - exp(-j 24pi t). How do I show an imaginary amplitude on the frequency spectrum? ?Wouldn't it just be easier to convert the sin(24pi t) into a cosine to avoid the 2j?

A term $a e ^ {j 2 \pi f _ o t}$ in a signal, where $a$ is a complex number, gives rise to a stem in the spectrum situated at frequency $f _ o$ with height $| a |$ and label $a$. Like, for example, $( 1 / 2 j ) e ^ {j 2 \pi ( 3 ) t}$ makes for a stem of height $1 / 2$ at frequency $3$ labeled either $1 / 2 j$ or $( 1 / 2 ) e ^ {- j \pi / 2}$ or $- j / 2$ or however you want to express $1 / 2 j$.
