Two of my intuition-type thoughts about convolution are:

= Convolution tends to smooth things out. I.e., $h * x$ will generally be smoother than either $h$ or $x$.

= If both $h$ and $x$ have finite duration, and their support intervals have length $P$ and $L$ respectively, then $h * x$ will also have finite duration, and its support interval will generally have length $P + L - 1$. Thus convolution tends to spread things out over time --- $h * x$ will generally be "more spread out" than either $h$ or $x$.
