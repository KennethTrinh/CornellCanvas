Is the following expression

\frac{\sin\left(kn\pi\right)}{kn\pi}

equivalent to

\delta\left[n\right]

for any integer k?

Yeah, but it's not completely rigorous. Usually you can avoid the $k = 0$ or $n = 0$ case by considering it separately during the calculation. Like for example

$\frac{1}{2 \pi} \int _ {- \pi} ^ {\pi} e ^ {j n \widehat{\omega}} d \widehat{\omega} = \left \{ \begin{array}{cl} 1 & {\rm when} \; n = 0 \\ \frac{\sin ( \pi n )}{\pi n} & {\rm when} \; n \neq 0 \; , \end{array} \right .$

which is the same as $\delta [ n ]$.
