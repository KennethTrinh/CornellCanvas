It might help you see what's going on to look at the convolution of a 4-point signal with a 5-point signal. Suffice it to say that when $x [ n ] = 0$ for $n < 0$, the ostensibly infinite sum

$\sum _ {k = 0} ^ \infty h [ k ] x [ n - k ]$

has at most one nonzero term, namely $h [ 0 ] x [ 0 ]$. And when $n = 1$ the sum has at most two nonzero terms ---- it equals $h [ 0 ] x [ 1 ] + h [ 1 ] x [ 0 ]$. Etc.
