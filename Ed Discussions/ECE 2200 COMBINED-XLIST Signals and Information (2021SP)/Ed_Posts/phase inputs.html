We solved the general form of the output to a pure frequency input: $$e^{jnw_0} \Longrightarrow \lVert H(w_0) \rVert e^{jnw_0} e^{\angle H(w_0)}$$ But what if the input had a phase, i.e. $$e^{jnw_0 + \phi_0}$$ Perhaps I just missed this in lecture, but what would the corresponding output frequency response be?

Author: David Delchamps (admin)

The phase just adds. One way to see this is linearity: $e ^ {j ( \widehat{\omega} _ o + \phi )} = e ^ {j \phi} e ^ {j \widehat{\omega _ o}}$ and you can think of the leading factor just as a multiplicative constant on the input.
