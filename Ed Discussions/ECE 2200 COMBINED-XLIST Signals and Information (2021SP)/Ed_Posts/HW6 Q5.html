If someone on Ho Plaza says a system's frequency response is

$\widehat{H} ( \widehat{\omega} ) = 3 - 7 e ^ {- j \widehat{\omega}}+ 13 e ^ {- j 7 \widehat{\omega}}$,

then I know right away that its impulse response is $h [ 0 ] = 3$, $h [ 1 ] = - 7$, $h [ 7 ] = 13$, and $h [ n ] = 0$ for all other $n$. Ii can figure that out either by reversing the process to get "from the $b _ k$ to the frequency response," or I can use the formula

$ h [ n ] = \frac{1}{2 \pi} \int _ {- \pi} ^ \pi \widehat{H} ( \widehat{\omega} ) e ^ {j n \widehat{\omega}} d \widehat{\omega}$.
