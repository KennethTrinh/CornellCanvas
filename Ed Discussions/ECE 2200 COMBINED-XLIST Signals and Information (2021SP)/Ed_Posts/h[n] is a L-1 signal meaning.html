What does it mean for h[n] to be a L-1 signal in the context of BIBO?

----------- REPLIES -----------

Author: David Delchamps (admin)

Vote Count: 1

$h [ n ]$ is an $l ^ 1$ signal means $\sum _ {n = - \infty} ^ \infty | h [ n ] |$ converges --- i.e. $h [ n ]$ is what people call an absolutely summable signal. In the context where $h [ n ]$ is the impulse response of a causal LTI system, the sum runs only from 0 to $\infty$ because $h [ n ] = 0$ for $n < 0$.
