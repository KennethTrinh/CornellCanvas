In what context do you mean "allowed to"? In this course, the standard form of a pure sinusoid is $A \cos ( 2 \pi f _ o t + \phi )$ with $f _ o \geq 0$. You can always put it in that form. For example, $\cos ( 2 \pi ( - 3 ) t + \pi / 7 ) = \cos ( 2 \pi ( 3 ) t - \pi / 7 )$. When I discussed principal continuous-time aliases and the recipe for finding the principal one, I assumed $f _ o \geq 0$.
