As I was grading Problem 4 on Homework 3, there was part of that problem that asked you to find the general complex coefficient of the sawtooth signal, which required integrating by parts. Many students came up with the right answer, although not simplified. If your answer was something similar to: ak = (-1 + (j2pik+ 1)e^-j2pik)/4(pik)^2 I will try and return points I may have taken off. We can evaluate this expression further using our knowledge of Euler's Identity, e^jtheta = cos(theta)+jsin(theta) If theta = 2pik where k is any integer, because cos and sin are 2pi periodic, theta can be reduced to 2pi, giving us e^j2pik = e^j2pi We follow this step by using Euler's identity e^j2pi = cos(2pi) + jsin(2pi) = 1 + 0 = 1 Therefore, e^j2pik, where k is any integer, is 1. In the case of this problem, simplifying ak will give you ak= j/2pik. Please let me know if anyone has questions about this, as it's a common identity that can simplify many problems.
