The phase of a complex number of the form $a e ^ {j \psi}$ is $\psi$ when $a > 0$ and $\psi \pm \pi$ when $a < 0$. I.e., a minus sign in the amplitude adds (or subtracts --- either way is fine) a $\pi$ from the "apparent" phase $\psi$.

In the example from class (the rudimentary high-pass filter), we're looking at $a ^ {j \psi}$ where $a = 2 \sin ( \widehat{\omega} / 2 )$ and $\psi = - \widehat{\omega} / 2 + \pi / 2$. Because $a > 0$ when $\widehat{\omega} > 0$ and $a < 0$ when $\widehat{\omega} < 0$, we have

$\phi ( \widehat{\omega} ) = \left \{ \begin{array}{cl} - \widehat{\omega} / 2 + \pi / 2 & {\rm when} \; \widehat{\omega} > 0 \\ - \widehat{\omega} / 2 - \pi / 2 & {\rm when} \; \widehat{\omega} < 0 \; . \end{array} \right .$
