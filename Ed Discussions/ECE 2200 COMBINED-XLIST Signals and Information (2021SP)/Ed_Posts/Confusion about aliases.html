Author: David Delchamps (admin)

Vote Count: 0

If $f _ s$ is a sampling frequency, every sinusoid of frequency $f _ o$ has infinitely many continuous-time aliases wrt $f _ s$ --- this is true for any $f _ o$. If you want to (a) $f _ s$-sample a sinusoid $x ( t )$ of frequency $f _ o$, then (b) put the resulting discrete-time signal through an ideal $T _ s$-interpolator, and finally (c) have the original sinusoid $x ( t )$ emerge, you have to make sure $f _ s > 2 f _ o$. The interpolator always puts out the principal continuous-time alias of $x ( t )$ wrt $f _ s$, and you need to make sure that $x ( t )$ itself is its own principal continuous-time alias wrt $f _ s$, and that's what the inequality $f _ s < 2 f _ o$, equivalently $f _ o < f _ s / 2$, ensures.
