Author: David Delchamps (admin)

Vote Count: 0

$\widehat{H} ( \widehat{\omega} )$ is defined for all $\widehat{\omega}$, but because it turns out to be a $2 \pi$-periodic function of $\widehat{\omega}$, people customarily graph it only on an interval of length $2 \pi$, which determines what it looks like for all $\widehat{\omega}$ by periodicity. $[ - \pi , \pi ]$ is the usual interval people use, although in a sense the DFT uses $[ 0 , 2 \pi ]$.

Author: David Delchamps (admin)

Vote Count: 0

Just look at the formula for it in terms of impulse response, namely

$\widehat{H} ( \widehat{\omega} ) = \sum _ {n = - \infty} ^ \infty h [ n ] e ^ {- j n \widehat{\omega}}$,

and you see that every term in the sum is $2 \pi$-periodic. BTW for a causal FIR system the sum is finite --- goes fron $n = 0$ to $n = M$ --- and the same reasoning applies.
