First of all, $a _ k = \frac{A _ k}{2} e ^ {j \phi _ k}$ and $x _ {- k} = \frac{A _ k}{2} e ^ {- j \phi _ k}$ when $1 \leq k \leq N$. Sorry if I got that wrong on the board --- I haven't checked the video.

For your other questions, let's see whether this helps. Suppose for simplicity $N = 2$. Then the last expression, i.e. the complex-exponential Fourier series for $x ( t )$, reads

$x ( t ) = a _ {- 2} e ^ {- j 2 ( 2 \pi ) f _ o t} + a _ {- 1} e ^ {- j 2 \pi f _ o t} + a _ 0 + a _ 1 e ^ {j 2 \pi f _ o t} + a _ 2 e ^ {j 2 ( 2 \pi ) f _ o t}$.

Thus $a _ {\pm 1}$ and $a _ {\pm 2}$ all appear in the sum.
