It's true for $0 \leq k < N / 2$ by definition of the $N/2$-point DFT of $x _ e [ n ]$. For $N / 2 \leq k < N$, you can re-write $k$ in the exponent as $N / 2 + \langle k \rangle _ {N / 2}$ and then use the fact that $\left ( \psi _ {N / 2} \right ) ^ {N / 2} = 1$. In class, I went over no such details, just made the leap. :-)
