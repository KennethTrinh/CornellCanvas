So this was something I also asked after class, but essentially, this is how it applies:

When you're finding the a_k, you the integral of (x(t) - 37)*e^stuff. This distributes to be x(t)*e^stuff and 37*e^stuff. From class, we know that the integral over T_0 of e^stuff is 0; and we can just pull out a constant from integration; resulting in the integral of 37*e^stuff = 0. Therefore, you're just left with the integral over T_0 of x(t)*e^stuff-- identical to our original x(t) a_k.
