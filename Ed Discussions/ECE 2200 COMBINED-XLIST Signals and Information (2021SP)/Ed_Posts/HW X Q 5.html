Here's how I approached this problem.

$H ( z ) = \frac{z ^ 4 - z ^ 3 + z ^ 2 - z + 1}{z ^ 4}$,

so we know it has a pole of order 4 at $z = 0$ and four zeros someplace. Plot #5 is the only one that works. As for actually finding the zeros, there's a formula for zeros of a degree-4 polynomial, but when I did this problem I didn't go there in the solutions.

One way to check the zeros is to look at the picture and see that it stipulates four zeros on the unit circle, i.e. four values of $\widehat{\omega}$ satisfying

$\sum _ {k = 0} ^ 4 \left ( - e ^ {j \widehat{\omega}} \right ) ^ k = 0$.

You can use the partial geometric series and usual messing around to turn this into

$\frac{\cos ( 5 \widehat{\omega} / 2 )}{\cos ( \widehat{\omega} / 2 )} = 0$,

so $\widehat{\omega}$ is $\pm \pi / 5$ and $\pm 3 \pi / 5$ (keeping it between $- \pi$ and $\pi$), and that matches the picture.
