$\widehat{X} [ k ] = \sum _ {n = 0} ^ 9 e ^ {- j n k 2 \pi / 10}$ for $0 \leq k < 10$. For $k = 0$, this equals 10. For $1 \leq k < 10$, partial geometric series yields $\widehat{X} [ k ] = \frac{1 - e ^ {- j k 2 \pi}}{1 - e ^ {- j k 2 \pi / N}} = 0$ because $e ^ {j k 2 \pi} = 1$. Another way to see it: the DTFT of the length-10 pulse is the Dirichlet thing $\widehat{X} ( \widehat{\omega} = \frac{\sin ( 5 \widehat{\omega} )}{\sin ( \widehat{\omega} / 2 )} e ^ {- j 5 \widehat{\omega}}$, and $\widehat{X} [ k ] = \widehat{X} ( k 2 \pi / 10 )$. Evaluate that for each $k$ and you get $\widehat{X} [ k ] = 10$ for $k = 0$ and zero for $1 \leq k < 10$. Consider $k = 0$ separately --- use L'Hôpital if you want.
