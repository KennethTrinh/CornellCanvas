No! The book does it that way --- "$N$-point signals are periodic" --- but I do it the following way: "an $N$-point signal is a whole signal $x [ n ]$ that satisfies $x [ n ] = 0$ for $n < 0$ and for $n \geq N$. This is probably my most major departure from how the book does DFT stuff, which I find kludgy.
