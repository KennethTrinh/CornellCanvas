Yeah, when you sample the first term in $x ( t )$ at 150 Hz and put the samples through the ideal D-to-C, it comes out as itself because of that inequality. Meanwhile, the second term samples to the discrete-time constant signal 1, which comes out of the ideal D-to-C as the continuous-time constant signal 1, which is the principal continuous-time alias of the second term in $x ( t )$ wrt sampling frequency 150 Hz.
