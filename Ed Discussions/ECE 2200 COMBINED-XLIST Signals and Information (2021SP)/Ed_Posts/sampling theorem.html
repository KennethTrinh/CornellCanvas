The ideal D-to-C operates in a specific way. If you sample a cosine $x ( t )$ and put the result through the ideal D-to-C, a cosine will indeed emerge --- it just might not be the same cosine as $x ( t )$. It will be the principal continuous-time alias of $x ( t )$ wrt the sampling frequency.
